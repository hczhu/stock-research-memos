# Single user survey in 2025-11
Is Figma still your main design tool? 
Yes.

Do you use any competing products? 
Not really. In fact, other parts of my organization are actually transitioning from Axure to Figma right now, so we are consolidating tools rather than picking up new ones.

Are there any emerging products in your designer circle?
I haven't seen any emerging products lately. Figma keeps expanding to include features from other tools, so I haven't looked elsewhere.

What do you like most about Figma? What makes it stand out from other tools?
Auto Layout, Components, and Community plugins.
Auto Layout: Makes designs cleaner and easier to adjust without needing to "push pixels."
Components/Libraries: Allows us to build assets efficiently and saves maintenance effort during brand updates.
Community: When native features are missing, the large user community helps bridge the gap with plugins.

Are Figma AI features helpful for you? Do you use ChatGPT’s Figma integration?
The AI feature I use most is actually just "Rename Layers" to keep files organized. Since my current role is high-level UX and we have well-defined design systems and content designers, generative features like "create an image" or "first draft" aren't very helpful to me, but I can see how those features would be great for early-stage ideation or visualizing early concepts.
