- ## Main Narrative
	- > What will the business look like in 5–10 years? Is the market big enough for 10x? Can it 4x revenue in 10 years?
	- **The one-line story**: *"Roblox is bigger than a gaming platform — it's a 3D communications platform."* The business is a two-sided UGC network (players + developers) compounding toward a 1-billion-user, all-ages, all-device, global platform, with monetization expanding from virtual goods into advertising and commerce.
	- **The four-dimensional growth strategy**: age up, everywhere (every device), international, and a vibrant economy. Road to 200M DAU and "connect 1 billion people."
	- **The age-up pivot (2026)**: The thesis is shifting from a kids' platform to an all-ages one. 18–34 users grew **+50% YoY** and monetize **50% higher** than under-18s (2026 Q1). O18 players represent **80% of the global gaming market (≈\$150B ex-China)**. Age verification (chat gated globally Jan 2026) is the near-term cost — it slowed new-user signups and **cut FY bookings growth guidance to 8–12%** — but gives Roblox its first accurate read on adult users, which it's using to raise creator payouts (**DevEx increasing 42% for age-verified O18 U.S. spend effective June 8, 2026**, up from 37.8% previously) to seed older-skewing content. As of Q1 2026, **26% of age-checked DAUs are O18**.
	- **One-of-a-kind, with no real substitute**: Roblox is a singular gaming business — from both the developer and player experience, there is no direct substitute. It uniquely combines a creator engine, distribution, social graph, and a full game-operating backend (hosting, matchmaking, payments, moderation) into one platform. The closest analogs each cover only a slice (Fortnite/UEFN on the engine, Steam on distribution, Minecraft on modding) — none replicate the end-to-end "create → publish → monetize → operate" loop at Roblox's scale and accessibility. See the Competition Landscape platform comparison below.
	- **The market's mental model is still outdated.** The market still prices Roblox with an old frame: kids play simple games → kids spend Robux → parents control the wallet → growth slows once kids saturate. The more accurate frame is: Roblox is a user-generated gaming platform with a large social graph, a native virtual economy, a creator marketplace, and it is expanding into young adult audiences. A kids-only platform has a low ceiling. An all-ages entertainment platform has a much higher ceiling. Young adult users can support more complex games, higher average spending, persistent social worlds, branded virtual goods, ads, commerce, subscriptions, premium creator economics, and more mature genres. Roblox does not need to abandon kids — it needs to keep kids while expanding into young adults. That is how the platform grows out of the "kids' game" box and why aging up changes the monetization model, not just the demographic mix.

	- **Why growth can re-accelerate (four reasons):**
		- 1. **Underlying engagement is already massive.** The question is not whether users want to spend time on Roblox — they already do. The question is whether Roblox can convert that engagement into more revenue per hour. Aging up directly addresses this.
		- 2. **Young adult mix shift can lift bookings without DAU growth.** If 18–34 users monetize 50% higher than under-18s, then audience-mix improvement alone can re-accelerate revenue growth even at flat total user counts. Better games attract more young adults. That is the supply-side flywheel.

Over time it becomes infrastructure: advertisers, brands, parents, regulators, and developers all want a safer, more predictable platform. - ## Innovative Culture
	- Roblox encourages **systems-level thinking** over one-off feature development; mantras "Take the Long View" and "Take Responsibility."
	- Heavy R&D culture: in-house world models, 400+ AI models, training NPCs on 13B hours of 3D world data; "Roblox Reality" (hyperscale multiplayer + photorealism + human-like NPCs).
	- **AI capability ramp (8-quarter trend)**: a steady escalation from moderation → discovery → generative worlds — AI-driven moderation (2024Q1) → open-sourced voice-safety model, 20k+ downloads (2024Q4) → 300+ ML models + in-experience LLM text API (2025Q1) → game foundation models + GPUs deployed in own data centers (2025Q3) → 400+ models, in-house world model, NPCs on 13B hrs of 3D data (2025Q4) → Roblox Reality / Video World Models (2026Q1). Each quarter compounds the platform's AI surface area — a consistent, accelerating R&D commitment.
	- **Roblox Reality — technical architecture (2026)**: The aging-up strategy is not just a demographic or marketing play; it is backed by a hard technical bet. Roblox Reality is a hybrid architecture announced in 2026 that combines the Roblox Game Engine and Roblox Cloud with a Roblox Video World Model ("Super Upsampler") to layer photorealistic visuals on top of structured game state. Roblox explicitly argues that *pure* video world models are not sufficient for real multiplayer games because they struggle with persistence, rule enforcement, long-term memory, user-input control, and consistent multiplayer simulation at scale. The architecture separates concerns: the game engine remains the source of truth for game logic and multiplayer state; the AI model generates the expensive visual layer — textures, lighting, fine-scale motion, fluid effects, background dynamics, and photorealistic rendering. In plain English: the engine manages the world; the AI makes the world look real. Development targets are 2K resolution at 60 Hz near-term, with a stated ambition of 4K at 60 Hz using H200/B200-class GPUs or equivalent accelerators in adjacent edge data centers. This runs on cloud-edge GPU infrastructure with the Video World Model deployed near users for latency performance.
	- **AI talent acquisitions (June 2026)**: Roblox announced the joining of founders and teams from three AI companies to accelerate the Roblox Reality vision: (1) **Morpheus AI** — work on *Self Forcing* to convert slow offline video models into faster autoregressive interactive generation engines; (2) **Dynamics Lab** — real-time generative world engines; (3) **Lucid AI** — balancing AI-generated visuals with deterministic game logic. The common theme: make AI-generated worlds interactive, low-latency, consistent, and game-like — not just visually impressive demos. This reinforces that Roblox is not treating AI as a single feature but as a full AI stack across the platform.
	- **Why this matters for aging up**: Older users will not stay on Roblox because the company adds age verification or pays developers more — they need better-looking games, deeper experiences, richer worlds, more believable NPCs, more responsive multiplayer systems, and content quality closer to mainstream gaming. Roblox Reality is the attempt to let small teams build experiences that look far more expensive than they actually are — democratizing photorealistic multiplayer gaming by reducing the dev time, cost, and compute normally required for high-fidelity graphics. If it works, the creator base expands upward: better tools attract better developers → better content → older users → better monetization → higher creator payouts → better content. That is the aging-up flywheel. Risk: Roblox Reality is early and Roblox itself says the current model does not run in real time in lab demos; cost-intensive scaling remains a challenge.
	- Positive-culture signals: weekly town halls, monthly CEO AMAs (David Baszucki), open lunches with leadership, Roblox Week, no-meeting days / core hours.

- ## Pre-Mortem — What Can Go Wrong?
	- 1. **Profitability / marginal costs** — structural take: ≈24% app-store fees + ≈27% to developers + ≈33% infra & safety = **≈84% of revenue**. The model only works if app-store fees fall and infra leverage improves.
	  2. **Saturation in high-ARPU countries** — US DAU ≈26M (Q3 2025); growth increasingly comes from lower-ARPU regions (APA 46M DAU at \$5.27 ABPDAU vs US \$40.18), pressuring blended monetization.
	  3. **Age-up execution risk** — verification slowed signups and cut guidance; if older cohorts don't convert to higher-margin content, the pivot stalls.
	  4. **Ads may not scale** — kid-safety constraints limit ad inventory/targeting; management itself said (2023) "we've never said advertising is going to be the meat of our business."
	  5. **VR substitution** — Meta Horizon Worlds and VR-native UGC could capture the next generation of under-13s.
	  6. **Founder-CEO age / succession** — David Baszucki born 1963 (founder-led is a strength, but key-person risk).
	  7. **Monetization ceiling** — if ARPU stays stuck near 4% MUP/MAU, the \$13.5B ARPU-parity upside never materializes. **Cautionary comp — Fortnite**: revenue peaked at ≈\$6B in 2021 (likely a COVID-boosted high) and has not reclaimed it since; Epic Games conducted [layoffs](https://www.epicgames.com/site/news/todays-layoffs) — evidence that even the closest competitor's UGC/live-service model can plateau and de-rate after a demand pull-forward. A reminder that gaming-platform engagement and spend can mean-revert.
	  8. **Recurring regulatory / geographic headwinds (8-quarter trend)** — friction shows up almost every year: Turkey ban (Aug 2024), Russia ban (Dec 2025), and the global age-verification rollout that gated chat, slowed new-user signups, and **cut FY2026 bookings guidance to 8–12%** (2025Q3, 2026Q1). A large, kid-heavy, global platform is a standing target for child-safety regulation and country bans — a persistent, partly-unpredictable drag on growth and a compliance-cost burden.

	- **Content-only (ex-hardware)** FY2026: Digital Software & Add-on Content (\$16.1B) + Network Services (\$5.1B) = **≈\$21.2B** — the figure most comparable to Roblox bookings and Steam revenue
	- **Total segment** FY2026: **≈\$30.5B** (incl. hardware) — the figure used in the "PlayStation comparison" engagement-vs-monetization point
	- **Read**: Sony's content+digital revenue grew (+5% digital software/add-on, +14% network services) while hardware fell (−12%) — the platform is mix-shifting toward higher-margin digital/services, the same direction Roblox monetization is heading.
