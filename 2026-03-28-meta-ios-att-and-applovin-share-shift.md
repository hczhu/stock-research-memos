# Meta iOS ATT Disruption and AppLovin Share Shift

Historically, Meta’s struggles on iOS trace back to Apple’s April 2021 launch of App Tracking Transparency (ATT), which required users to opt in to sharing their unique device identifier, the IDFA. Before this shift, Meta dominated iOS by combining IDFA data with its own platform signals to power effective targeting and lookalike models.

When ATT rolled out, Meta’s iOS revenue collapsed almost overnight. In some cases, daily revenue fell from tens of thousands of dollars to nearly zero as the core signals on which its systems depended disappeared.

While competitors like AppLovin moved quickly to develop alternative models to restore performance, Meta has spent the past four and a half years promising a non-ATT tracking framework that has yet to arrive. That delay gave AppLovin the opportunity to capture Meta’s former market share, leaving Meta stuck in a cycle of promising publishers it will return to iOS while AppLovin continues to dominate.
