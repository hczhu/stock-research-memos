# Google Maps EU DMA Traffic Effects (2024 DiD)

In January 2024, Google changed how location-based queries were shown to EU users by removing clickable maps and direct links to Google Maps. This policy-induced change enables a difference-in-differences (DiD) comparison between EU and non-EU countries to evaluate how removing Google self-preferencing affected search volume and traffic patterns.

Key findings:

- Searches for `maps` and `google maps` rose by more than 21%.
- While `maps` could reflect broader mapping interest, `google maps` more directly indicates intent to use Google Maps.
- The increase in search activity largely redirected users back to Google Maps.
- Traffic data show no significant change in total visits to `www.google.com/maps`, on desktop or mobile.
- Instead, the evidence points to shifts in access channels and session duration.
- No corresponding increase appears in search or traffic for Bing Maps or other competing mapping services.

Conclusion:

- The DMA intervention had weak competitive effects in this context.
- Google Maps’ market position remained dominant, with alternatives still limited.
