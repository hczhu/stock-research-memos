# Jagged Technological Frontier: AI's Impact on Knowledge Worker Productivity

## Core Concept

AI capabilities form a "jagged technological frontier" — uneven across task types. AI may excel at complex tasks while failing at others that appear equally difficult. Effective use requires navigating this frontier: knowing which tasks to delegate to AI and which to keep for humans.

## Key Findings (GPT-4 Study on Consultants)

Source: Working paper "Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier"

### Inside the Frontier (Tasks AI Can Do Well)

For tasks within AI's capabilities (creative product innovation, writing, analytical tasks):

- **Productivity**: AI-assisted consultants completed `12.2%` more tasks and were `25.1%` faster
- **Quality**: Work quality scores increased by more than `40%`
- **Skill leveling**: Lower-baseline performers saw a `43%` improvement vs. `17%` for top performers — AI disproportionately lifted weaker performers

### Outside the Frontier (Tasks AI Handles Poorly)

For tasks requiring subtle interpretation of qualitative and quantitative data:

- AI-assisted consultants were `19 percentage points` less likely to produce correct solutions vs. those working without AI
- Over-reliance leads to blind adoption of incorrect AI outputs, degrading human performance

## Emerging Integration Patterns

**Centaurs**: Strategically divide labor — delegate specific sub-tasks to AI, handle others independently. Clean separation of human vs. AI work.

**Cyborgs**: Fully integrate AI into workflow at a granular level — co-writing sentences, iterating on ideas in tandem. Continuous interaction rather than task handoffs.

## Homogenization Risk

AI improved average quality of creative ideas but reduced **diversity** of output. AI-assisted work was more similar across participants than work done independently. This is a systemic risk for organizations relying heavily on AI for ideation.

## Investment Relevance

- Productivity gains are real and large, but unevenly distributed across task types
- The skill-leveling effect has implications for labor markets: AI may compress the wage premium for mid-tier knowledge workers
- Homogenization of AI-generated output could create opportunities for differentiated, human-led creative work
- Enterprises that teach employees *when not to use AI* may outperform those that push blanket AI adoption
