# McKinsey Attention Equation for Consumer Media Monetisation (Exhibit 1)

Context: McKinsey exhibit on how to better explain `$ per hour viewed` by incorporating attention quality, not just monetization mechanics.

## Extracted Equation

`$ per hour viewed = commercial quotient + attention quotient`

Equivalent decomposition shown in the exhibit:

`$ per hour viewed = (Consumer value × Platform sophistication) + (Focus × Intent)`

## Components

### Commercial quotient (about 2/3 of explanatory power)

1. **Consumer value**
   - Economic worth of consumers for a given medium, including:
     - Average income
     - Percent of “super spenders” encompassed
     - Age (younger = higher value)
     - Receptivity to advertising

2. **Platform sophistication**
   - Effectiveness of the platform where consumption occurs, including:
     - Industry structure
     - Advertising availability and integration
     - Data availability and technological sophistication

### Attention quotient (about 1/3 of explanatory power)

3. **Focus**
   - Level of focus while consuming media (e.g., degree of multitasking, mind wandering).

4. **Intent**
   - Purpose of consumption, defined by five potential “jobs to be done” by media.

Source context shown on exhibit:
- Kabir Ahuja, Marc Brodherson, Jamie Vickers, and Jordan Bank, *The ‘attention equation’: Winning the right battles for consumer attention*, McKinsey, June 10, 2025.
