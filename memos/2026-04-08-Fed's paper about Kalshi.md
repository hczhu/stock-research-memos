- This paper, [Kalshi and the Rise of Macro Markets](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/files/2026010pap.pdf), evaluates the utility of prediction markets—specifically the CFTC-regulated platform **Kalshi**—as a tool for measuring real-time macroeconomic expectations.
  
  The authors present the following main arguments and findings:
- ### 1. Superior Real-Time Data Source
  
  The authors argue that traditional tools like surveys (which become stale quickly) and financial derivatives (which are often illiquid or complex) have significant drawbacks. Kalshi offers **high-frequency, continuously updated** data that provides a "distributionally rich" benchmark for both researchers and policymakers.
- ### 2. High Forecasting Accuracy
  
  The study finds that Kalshi's forecasting performance is highly competitive:
- **Federal Funds Rate:** Kalshi’s "mode" forecast perfectly predicted the FOMC outcome on the day of the meeting in every instance since 2022, slightly outperforming fed funds futures.
- **Inflation:** For headline CPI, Kalshi provided a statistically significant improvement over the **Bloomberg consensus** forecast.
- **Labor Market:** Expectations for unemployment and payrolls were found to be as accurate as professional surveys.
- ### 3. Filling Information "Gaps"
  
  Kalshi provides distributional forecasts (full probability densities) for variables where options data were historically unavailable, such as:
- **Real GDP Growth**
- **Core CPI**
- **Unemployment Rate**
- **Specific FOMC meeting decisions** (standard options often average over several months/meetings).
- ### 4. Responsiveness to News
  
  The paper documents that these markets respond "sharply and sensibly" to intraday developments. For example:
- **Asymmetric Responses:** Positive CPI surprises lead to much larger shifts in interest rate expectations than negative surprises.
- **Uncertainty Resolution:** The variance of interest rate distributions typically declines following major data announcements (especially inflation), signaling a resolution of market uncertainty.
- ### 5. Unique Retail Perspective
  
  Unlike institutional-heavy markets (like SOFR options), Kalshi attracts retail investors. The authors suggest this provides a "cleaner" lens on expectations, as retail participants are less likely to be driven by the complex hedging motives or portfolio constraints that can distort institutional market prices.
