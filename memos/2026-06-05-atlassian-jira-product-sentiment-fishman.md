- ## Jira Deserves Less Hatred, Not More Love (Fishman AF Newsletter)
	- **Source**: Fishman AF Newsletter, Adam Fishman, "Hot take alert: I have some feelings" (fishmanafnewsletter.com)
	- **Note**: This is a **product/UX piece — no stock, financials, or valuation content**. Useful for the Vibe Checks / competitive-moat read, not for Napkin Math.
	- **Thesis (the author's)**: Jira's bad reputation is partly undeserved — it has real UX flaws, but every competitor is "equally problematic," so Jira persists as market leader through *relative parity, not superiority*. "Jira deserves less hatred, not more love."

- ## User Sentiment — Hate Is Loud but Shallow

	| Data point | Value |
	|---|---|
	| Dislike Jira | ~**1/3** of surveyed users |
	| Indifferent or satisfied | **64%** |

	- The "Jira is hated" narrative (memes, dedicated hate sites) overstates churn risk — most users are *fine* with it; experts say it works well **when properly configured**

- ## Jira — Strengths
	- **Core competency = delivery**: basic bug and task management — "It's great for engineers"
	- **Market dominance**: the "vast, vast majority" of tech companies use it
	- **Super customizable**: flexible workflow design
	- **Works adequately out of the box**

- ## Jira — Weaknesses
	- **UX/design**: the "Winchester Mystery House of software" — excessive menus, icons, unintelligible elements
	- **Scope creep**: poor fit for product work *beyond delivery* (ideation, design, debate, research)
	- **Integration friction**: a "colossal mess" that forces users to link external Google Docs
	- **Confluence** inadequate for collaborative writing and access management

- ## Competitive Position — Everyone Else Is Also Flawed
	- Notion: "terrible for writing," becomes a "document farm"
	- Trello (Atlassian-owned): "basically unusable" at scale
	- Linear, Coda, Monday, Asana, Basecamp, etc.: "all bother me in some way"
	- **Conclusion**: Jira keeps the lead because alternatives are no better, not because it's loved

- ## Investment Implications ($TEAM)
	- **Counters the "Jira-is-hated → churn/Linear-disruption" bear narrative**: 64% indifferent-or-satisfied + "works well when configured" supports the stickiness/switching-cost moat (consistent with the thesis's "hate is fashionable but rarely terminal" read and the RepVue/HN anecdotes in [[Atlassian-TEAM-thesis]])
	- **Relative-parity moat**: leadership held via entrenched workflow + no clearly-better alternative — durable but unexciting; the flip side is that the door stays open for a genuinely superior product (Linear at dev teams) since love, not just adequacy, drives the most motivated switchers
	- **Confluence is the softer flank**: weak for collaborative writing/access management — the place Notion/others chip at, and where Rovo/AI must improve
	- **Not a valuation input**: no financial data in the source — this informs product/competitive sections only
