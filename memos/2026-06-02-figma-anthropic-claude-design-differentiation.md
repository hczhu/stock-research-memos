- ## Figma Differentiation Against Anthropic / Claude Design
	- **Source**:
		- user-provided excerpt from Figma founder CEO's answer to a competition question on the `2026 Q1 earnings call`
		- the question context was perceived competition from AI labs and products such as Anthropic's `Claude Design`
	- **Thesis**:
		- Figma is not dismissing Anthropic because Anthropic can pair first-party models with first-party products.
		- Figma's defense is not model ownership; it is product-surface depth, multiplayer canvas mechanics, workflow context, and fast movement across design, code, AI, and direct manipulation.
	- **Context**:
		- Figma appears to view Google differently from Anthropic: Google is framed more as a partner through Gemini, while Anthropic is framed as a potential product competitor.
		- The CEO's answer implies that model quality alone is not the full competitive axis in design tools.
		- The key question is whether AI-native products can replicate Figma's collaborative canvas, workflow context, and design-to-code platform depth.

- ## Differentiation Table
	| Differentiator | CEO's argument | Why it matters against Anthropic / Claude Design | Investment read-through |
	| --- | --- | --- | --- |
	| `Performant multiplayer canvas` | Figma has spent years developing high-quality real-time canvas mechanics. | Design work is collaborative, visual, stateful, and latency-sensitive; a chat-first AI product has to replicate these mechanics to replace Figma. | Supports Figma's product moat in collaborative creative workflows, not just UI design files. |
	| `Canvas expertise beyond design` | The CEO argues that multiplayer canvas mechanics can apply to many representations, including code in the future. | Anthropic can generate artifacts, but Figma is positioning the canvas as a broader interface for manipulating design and code together. | Expands the TAM narrative from design tool to collaborative product-building workspace. |
	| `Deep product context from workflows` | Figma can build context from user workflows and long-running product usage. | Claude Design may have strong model capabilities, but Figma owns granular workflow context inside teams, files, comments, design systems, and collaboration patterns. | Workflow data and embedded usage may help Figma deliver more relevant AI assistance than generic model tools. |
	| `Team understanding of users` | Figma's team has accumulated context from serving designers and product teams over many years. | Anthropic has model expertise, but design-product judgment and user empathy are harder to acquire quickly. | Reinforces a product-craft moat rather than a pure technology moat. |
	| `One platform for AI, design, code, and direct manipulation` | Figma wants to combine AI, design, code, and freeform direct manipulation in one place. | Claude Design may compete on AI generation, but Figma is arguing that flow comes from moving rapidly between modalities inside one platform. | The more workflows stay inside Figma, the more pricing power and retention Figma may preserve. |
	| `Rapid movement between modalities` | The CEO says the ultimate differentiator is moving quickly between AI, design, code, and direct manipulation. | This reframes the competition from "who generates a screen" to "who owns the full iterative loop." | If true, Figma can remain the system of engagement even if foundation models commoditize parts of creation. |

	| Competitor / partner | CEO framing | Implication |
	| --- | --- | --- |
	| `Google / Gemini` | Figma is thankful for the partnership and sees opportunity to collaborate. Google's labs product is not seen as a substantial blocker so far. | Google is framed more as an ecosystem and model partner than as the primary near-term competitive threat. |
	| `Anthropic / Claude Design` | Figma cannot dismiss Anthropic because it can train first-party models and couple them with its own products. | Anthropic is a credible product threat because it can vertically integrate model capability and application UX. |

- ## Investment Implications
	- Figma's moat argument is primarily about workflow ownership and collaborative product depth, not proprietary foundation models.
	- The most important product claim is that a performant multiplayer canvas is hard to replicate and may matter beyond design, especially if code becomes another canvas-native representation.
	- Anthropic is still a real threat because first-party model ownership can compress product cycles and enable tight model-product coupling.
	- Figma's best defense is to make AI additive to its existing system of work, so users move faster inside Figma rather than leaving for a standalone AI generation tool.
	- The bull case depends on Figma owning the iterative loop across design, code, AI generation, review, and direct manipulation.
	- The bear case is that model-native products reduce the importance of Figma's current workflow context by creating a new default surface for product ideation.

- ## Memo Takeaway
	- Figma's answer to `Claude Design` is: models matter, but the hard product problem is owning the collaborative, contextual, multi-modal work surface.
	- The CEO's strongest differentiation claims are `performant multiplayer canvas`, `deep workflow context`, and the ability to move quickly between `AI`, `design`, `code`, and `direct manipulation` in one platform.
