- ## Memory Supply Status by Category — 2026
	- **Source**: Industry summary table, April 2026

- ## Supply Status Table

	| Category | Primary Tech | Dominant Vendors | 2026 Supply Status | The "Why" Behind the Shortage |
	|---|---|---|:---:|---|
	| AI & Data Center | HBM3E, HBM4, CXL | SK Hynix, Micron, Samsung | **SOLD OUT** | Production fully allocated through 2026–2027. HBM4 validation expected by 2Q26. |
	| Mobile & Phone | LPDDR5X, UFS 4.0 | Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron | **CRITICAL** | Contract prices for LPDDR5X surged ~90% QoQ as wafers are diverted to AI servers. |
	| PC & Server | DDR5, NVMe SSDs | Samsung, Micron, SK Hynix, WD | **EXPENSIVE** | Mainstream DDR5 prices jumped 60–95% because AI servers require massive system memory pools. |
	| Graphics & GPU | GDDR6, GDDR7 | Samsung, Micron, SK Hynix | **CONSTRAINED** | GDDR7 can reach 48 Gbps, but vendors are limiting capacity to focus on high-margin HBM. |
	| Legacy Tech | DDR4, MLC NAND | Nanya, Winbond, Samsung | **END-OF-LIFE** | Samsung ending MLC NAND production in 2026, causing a 40% drop in global capacity. |
	| Embedded / IoT | SRAM, eFlash, MRAM | STMicro, NXP, Renesas, Infineon | **TIGHT** | Lead times for automotive/industrial grades increased significantly as legacy lines are deprioritized. |

- ## Key Data Points
	- HBM (AI & Data Center): fully sold out through 2026–2027; HBM4 validation by 2Q26
	- LPDDR5X (mobile): contract prices +~90% QoQ — collateral damage from AI wafer diversion
	- DDR5 (PC & server): prices +60–95% from AI server system memory demand
	- GDDR7: technically capable (48 Gbps) but vendors starving it of capacity to maximize HBM margins
	- MLC NAND: Samsung exit in 2026 → 40% global capacity drop; end-of-life dynamic, not a demand surge
	- Embedded/IoT: lead times stretching as fabs deprioritize legacy nodes

- ## Investment Implications
	- The shortage is not uniform — it cascades from AI HBM demand crowding out wafer capacity across every other memory category
	- Mobile (LPDDR5X) and PC (DDR5) price spikes are a secondary effect, not primary demand — this could reverse sharply if AI capex slows
	- GDDR7 constraint is a vendor choice, not a structural shortage — HBM margin is simply too attractive to divert capacity
	- MLC NAND end-of-life is a one-time supply event; 40% capacity drop will reprice legacy storage markets
	- Embedded/IoT tightness is a quiet side effect that affects automotive and industrial supply chains — relevant for STMicro, NXP, Renesas, Infineon
	- The entire table resolves to one root cause: HBM is consuming wafer capacity that would otherwise serve every other memory segment
