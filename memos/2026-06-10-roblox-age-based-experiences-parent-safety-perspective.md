- ## The New Age-Tiered Account Structure (rolling out now)
	| Tier | Target age | Game access | Communication default | Notes |
	|---|---|---|---|---|
	| **Roblox Kids** | **5–8** | More **limited** selection of games | **Communication disabled by default** | Most controlled tier |
	| **Roblox Select** | **9–15** | **Wider** selection of age-appropriate games | Controlled framework (tighter than standard) | Middle tier |
	| **Standard Roblox** | older / age-checked | Full catalog | Standard | Default for teens/adults |
	- Design intent: stop "treating every child on the platform the same" → serve **age-appropriate games & features** per tier.
	- **Parental controls** now span: **content, communication, spending, screen time, and specific games.** Parents can **allow/block specific games regardless of the game's content-maturity label** (Allowed/Blocked Games feature).

- ## Trusted Connections & Facial Age Estimation
	- **Trusted Connections**: lets **age-checked users aged 13+** connect and chat **more freely with people they know**; **age groups determine which features are available.**
	- **Facial Age Estimation (FAE)**: the system that sorts users into age bands — and the gating mechanism for features like **Voice Chat**.
	- **Birthday accuracy is now the linchpin** — because features unlock off the age band, a wrong birthday puts the account in the wrong tier "before you even begin." **Linked parents can update a child's birthday only once**, and some restrictions apply → high-stakes, low-reversibility setting.

- ## Key Anecdote — FAE False-Positive (age inflation)
	- At **end of 2025**, Roblox's **Facial Age Estimation put Emma's then-9-year-old into the 13–15 category.**
	- This **instantly unlocked features the family had deliberately decided against — including Voice Chat.**
	- Takeaway she draws: **age checks/safety systems help but are not a substitute for a parent reviewing the account.** (Investor read-through: FAE has a non-trivial false-positive rate that can *over*-grant access — a regulatory/headline risk vector even as the system is net-positive for compliance.)

- ## Parent Sentiment & the "Platform, Not a Game" Framing
	- **"Roblox is a hard no from me"** — a common parental reflex, driven by headlines, Robux begging, sketchy game names (e.g. **"Steal a Brainrot"**), chat boxes, scams, and the feeling the child knows the platform far better than the parent.
	- Root cause of fear = **lack of understanding**, not anti-tech stance: parents don't know *who the child talks to, what they click, how the money works, which games are okay, which settings matter.*
	- **Roblox is a platform, not one game** — one child builds a house / designs outfits / plays a gentle obby; another is in a game "pushing too hard for money, attention or access." Same brand, very different experiences/risk.
	- **Creative-platform upside** (often missed by parents): kids can **build & design games, learn basic coding logic, make outfits, understand digital money, run communities, and sometimes earn** → a first step into design/coding/entrepreneurship/storytelling/collaboration.
	- Emma's stance = **"a guided yes"** (middle ground between ban and free rein): *"Move over. Show me how to play."* Practical tactic: ask the child to demo account setup so the parent builds their own linked parent account alongside, reframing a controls audit as collaboration rather than confiscation.

- ## Investment Implications (read-through to thesis)
	- **Age-tiering = monetization + durability enabler**: Kids/Select segmentation + granular parental controls (spending, screen time, allowed/blocked games) is the infrastructure that lets Roblox **defend the under-13 base and "age up" teens** into higher-monetizing, communication-enabled experiences — supportive of the [[2026-06-08-roblox-aging-up-thesis]].
	- **Trust & safety as regulatory moat**: Trusted Connections + FAE + default-off comms for young kids are the compliance posture that **lowers regulatory/ban risk** (a key tail risk for $RBLX). But the FAE false-positive anecdote shows the system can **over-grant access (e.g. Voice Chat to a 9-yo)** — a live headline/regulatory risk if mis-aging is widespread.
	- **Engagement-quality angle**: the parent-education push (Parent Champions, newsletters) is part of converting "hard no" households into "guided yes" households — **expanding the addressable parent-gated audience** rather than just defending it.
	- **Watch items**: FAE accuracy / false-positive rate; adoption & friction of the one-time-only birthday correction; whether Select's "wider age-appropriate catalog" meaningfully lifts engagement/bookings for the 9–15 cohort.
