- ## Neocloud Revenue Dreams
- Projected revenue for upstart GPU cloud providers (2025–2028), including Nscale's scenario if it acquires a major U.S. data center campus (AIP acquisition).
- Source: The Information Reporting, Goldman Sachs and FactSet
- ### Projected Revenue ($B)
- | Company | 2025 | 2026 | 2027 | 2028 |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | CoreWeave | $5B | $12B | $20B | $29B |
  | Nscale | ~$0.2B | ~$1.5B | ~$9B | $12B |
  | Nebius | ~$0.3B | ~$4B | ~$8B | $10B |
  | Crusoe | ~$0.1B | ~$2B | ~$5B | $10B |
  | Nscale (after AIP acquisition)* | — | ~$2B | ~$30B | $36B |
- *These numbers are estimated pending acquisition. The AIP acquisition scenario assumes Nscale acquires a major U.S. data center campus, projecting a sharp jump from ~$9B to ~$30B in 2027 vs. the base case.
- ### Key observations
	- CoreWeave is the clear leader, reaching $29B by 2028 from a $5B base in 2025
	- Nscale, Nebius, and Crusoe converge around $10–12B by 2028 in base case scenarios
	- The AIP acquisition scenario would make Nscale the largest neocloud by 2028 ($36B), surpassing CoreWeave
	- The acquisition impact is most dramatic in 2027 (~$30B vs. ~$9B base case), a ~3x step-up
