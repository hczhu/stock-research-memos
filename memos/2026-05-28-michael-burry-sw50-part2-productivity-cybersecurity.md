- ## SW50 Part II: Productivity Tools & Cybersecurity — Michael Burry Analysis
	- **Source**: Michael Burry, Cassandra Unchained, "50 Software & Payments Stocks Part II: Productivity Tools and Cybersecurity", May 28, 2026
	- **Thesis**: Most productivity and cybersecurity stocks are trading at pre-subscription-era multiples while AI threatens some business models and strengthens others. ADBE is the lone Fat Pitch; cybersecurity names carry OLOL structural risk. Burry's classification system: Castle > Chapel > Stone; valuation zones: Fat Pitch < 2x IV15, Just Outside 2–4x, Out Field > 4x.

- ## Notes by Company

	- ### Adobe (ADBE) — Fat Pitch, Full Position
		- Stock at ~$248, down 65% from 2021 high of $688; trades at 7-year price lows and 14-year sales multiple lows
		- CEO fired after 18 years; no named successor — kitchen-sink quarter likely ahead, then reset
		- Firefly generative credit consumption +45% QoQ; video generative actions +8x YoY; Firefly ARR +75% QoQ
		- 850M MAUs growing 17% YoY; 99 of Fortune 100 customers; Firefly Foundry trains on customer brand IP
		- Moat: chain-of-IP-custody and licensed brand libraries — AI agents must go through Adobe to access authorized brand IP
		- $25B buyback authorized vs $101B market cap; below IV15 so buybacks are accretive
		- ΔE 88.3% (retains 88¢ per GAAP dollar after full SBC cost)
		- Burry defends against bear case: LLMs are not reason engines; creative human instinct is irreplaceable; Figma loss in hindsight a lucky escape as XD was structurally AI-vulnerable anyway

	- ### Intuit (INTU) — Castle, Watching at 1.5x IV15
		- TurboTax + QuickBooks near-utility status; Credit Karma growing 23%; launching QuickBooks for construction ($90B TAM)
		- TurboTax Live now 53% of TurboTax revenue; AI accounting agents serving 3M+ customers with 85%+ repeat engagement
		- AI agent finds avg $1,000 incremental deductions; stock-basis agent lowers taxable income avg $12,000
		- 17% workforce reduction; guiding to mid-teens EPS growth; Intuit Accountant Suite targeting top-20 accounting firms
		- Castle moat: proprietary Financial LLM never leaves walls; liability transactor — customers buy confidence, not code
		- ΔE 75.4% (just ok); buybacks at ~$400/share still above IV15 ($114), diluting intrinsic value per share
		- Burry wants to buy at ~1.5x IV15 (~$171); not close at 2.81x

	- ### Autodesk (ADSK) — Chapel, Admirer, Waiting for Down Cycle
		- AECO and manufacturing design incumbent; Civil 3D controls road building, Revit controls building construction
		- Revenue growing mid-teens; billings up 30%; 7% sales force reduction just completed (~$100M charge)
		- ΔE 103.5% — second only to Veeva in SW50; buybacks more than offset SBC over 11 years
		- Burry grid buyback mechanic: buys more shares when price falls (Q4: $333M at avg ~$303)
		- Risk: open partnership strategy with frontier models is more exposed than Intuit's walled Castle
		- Liability lock-in: engineers-of-record are personally licensed and liable; Autodesk seat is load-bearing until insurance changes
		- Data center buildout tailwind: hyperscaler construction consumes capacity, creating backlog for ADSK customers
		- Not cheap at 3.58x IV15 ($66); hopes to revisit in next down cycle

	- ### Unity Software (U) — Castle, Would Buy at 1x IV15
		- ~70% mobile game engine market share; turnaround under CEO Bromberg (ex-EA, 2 years in)
		- Q1 FY2026: revenue +17% YoY; strategic revenue +35%; EBITDA margin 27%; Vector ad revenue +80%
		- IronSource ($4.4B acquisition) fully shut down; replaced by Vector (ML-powered ad targeting)
		- Unity AI in beta: trained on 20 years of game data; Unity 6 engine downloads at 6-year-high pace
		- Moat: engine + Vector + Unity AI + commerce platform = integrated flywheel no third-party world model can replicate
		- SBC falling from 30% → 15% of revenue, headed toward 10%
		- Tragic Algebra: deeply negative GAAP history; ΔE not calculable; inflecting toward GAAP profitability later this year
		- Would buy at 1x P/IV15 ($13.03); current 2.13x still too rich

	- ### DocuSign (DOCU) — Stone, Do Not Own
		- eSignature market flattened; DNR only 102% (normal range 112–119%) for 6 consecutive quarters
		- IAM (Intelligent Agreement Management) generating $350M ARR after 18 months; 200M contracts in Navigator
		- SBC discipline: CFO celebrated flat SBC as "not bad" — Burry gives it an F for shareholder bucket
		- ΔE deeply negative; cumulative SBC $2.39B vs $1.35B cumulative GAAP income since 2023
		- Buyback at 4.26x IV15 ($11.37) is anti-shareholder — reduces intrinsic value per share
		- Competition: Adobe Acrobat Sign (zero marginal cost for existing Adobe customers), Microsoft 365 Copilot, IronClad, SpotDraft
		- Stone reflects: eSignature structurally threatened; IAM bet real but uncertain and already priced in or above

	- ### Zscaler (ZS) — Chapel, Watching Post-Earnings Drop
		- Zero Trust Exchange across ~160 data centers; 9,400 enterprise customers; 500B daily transactions
		- Revenue growth slowing: high-teens this year → mid-teens next; AI Protect bookings crossed $100M TTM
		- Acquiring Symmetry Systems (identity-to-data-source mapping for agent-to-agent security)
		- Mythos mentioned 6 times on earnings call; CEO sees frontier AI as "strongest tailwind ever"
		- OLOL (One Lake One Life) risk: all 9,400 customer traffic flows through one cloud — fatted calf for attackers
		- SBC at 22% of revenue (vs PANW 13%, dropping); GAAP net loss widening; owners' earnings ~-$200M
		- Two key sales execs departed; "prudent" guidance; capex rising, FCF declining
		- Was at $185 before earnings drop to ~$126; still not actionable at 5.85x IV15

	- ### Palo Alto Networks (PANW) — Castle, Do Not Own
		- Most complete cybersecurity platform; 65K+ firewall customers; 1,550 Prisma platformizations (+35% YoY)
		- XSIAM (2022): AI-native security ops replacing legacy SIEM; AgentiX runs on-prem to find/fix vulnerabilities
		- CyberArk acquired ~$25B (identity security for AI agents logging in at machine speed); Chronosphere (observability); Koi (agentic endpoint)
		- Glasswing (Anthropic project): Mythos given to 50 critical infrastructure orgs — PANW produced 5x normal security patches
		- Burry concern: Glasswing makes the fatted calf fatter; Mythos now knows vulnerabilities of largest AI orgs; model weights could be stolen
		- SBC 13.1% of revenue (best in cybersecurity, falling); organic growth fell 29% → 14%; CyberArk/Chronosphere acquisitions mask organic slowdown
		- Owners' earnings ~-$700M despite reported GAAP profitability (FCF absorbed by SBC dilution)
		- M&A treadmill reducing ROIC; still OLOL architecture; Castle due to platformization flywheel and 15PB data moat

	- ### CrowdStrike (CRWD) — Castle (last in SW50), Has Considered Shorting
		- Last (50/50) in SW50; trading at all-time highs despite being the most expensive and most dilutive
		- Falcon platform: single agent, one cloud, 29 modules, Falcon Flex (consumption-based pricing); 30K customers
		- July 2024 outage: crashed 8-9M Windows machines globally — proven OLOL structural fragility
		- OLOL proudly advertised as moat: 1T security events/day, 2T vertices, 15PB of data
		- Glasswing/Mythos: same OLOL-fattening risk as PANW; Threat Graph is exactly what attackers would want
		- SBC worst in class: 22.8% of revenue, rising; shares outstanding +25% over 5 years (4.5% annual dilution)
		- Owners' earnings ~-$1.4B on $4.93B revenue; $7B+ annual dilution value exceeds total revenue
		- ΔE not computable; Tragic Algebra cartoonishly negative across 7 years
		- Burry: "investors have fallen in love with a well-dressed solitary point of failure"

- ## Key Framework: OLOL vs SDDD
	- **OLOL (One Lake One Life)**: Current SASE/cloud security model — all customer data aggregated in one cloud; trains shared detection models; efficient flywheel but catastrophic single point of failure
	- **SDDD (Segregated and Deception-Defended Data)**: Burry's proposed next-gen architecture — isolated customer data pools + active deception/misdirection strategies; harder to build on legacy cash flows due to Innovator's Dilemma
	- Glasswing (Anthropic): giving Mythos-class model to 50 largest AI infrastructure orgs for "hardening" — Burry views this as training the exact model that makes OLOL architecture most vulnerable
	- PANW/CRWD both have pieces (AgentiX, deception tech, CyberArk identity) but neither has committed to wholesale SDDD transition

- ## Investment Implications
	- ADBE is the only Fat Pitch in this cohort (0.91x IV15); CEO uncertainty = near-term entry point, but moat is durable
	- Cybersecurity names (ZS, PANW, CRWD) carry compounding risk: OLOL architecture + brutal SBC + Mythos/Glasswing dynamic; PANW and CRWD at Out Field valuations
	- INTU Castle moat strongest in productivity (liability transactor, proprietary FinLLM, 88% TAM is assisted/human-confidence-driven); not cheap at 2.81x
	- ADSK has best shareholder alignment (ΔE 103.5%) but structurally more open than Intuit to frontier model disruption; wait for down cycle
	- CRWD most dangerous combination: worst SBC discipline + highest valuation + proven OLOL structural fragility + Glasswing risk; Burry has considered short
