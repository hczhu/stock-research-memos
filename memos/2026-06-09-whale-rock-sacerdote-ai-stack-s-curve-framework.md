- ## Whale Rock (Alex Sacerdote) — AI Stack, S-Curves & Hardware De-Commoditization
	- **Source**: *Invest Like the Best* with Patrick O'Shaughnessy — Alex Sacerdote, Founder of Whale Rock Capital Management (podcast, June 2026)
	- **Who**: Whale Rock = tech-focused fund, **>$17B AUM** across hedge fund / long-only / hybrid; **~44%/yr** compounding over the past 3 years. 20-yr tech track record, 10-person team.
	- **Framework (the entire firm in one lens)**: **(1) S-curve** (right part of the adoption curve → exponential unit growth) + **(2) durable competitive advantage** (moat) + **(3) underappreciated long-term earnings power** (earnings go $1→$10 far more often than the market prices). The combination lets you buy best-in-class compounders at low P/Es.

- ## Highest-Conviction Position: Anthropic (the AI-stack thesis)
	- **Jensen's 5-layer stack**: power → chips → clouds → foundation models → applications. Whale Rock's sequencing: **chips/infra first** (demand arrives first; winners knowable regardless of who wins above), then the model layer once it clarified, applications last.
	- **Model layer = oligopoly, not commodity**: April 2023 webinar framed 4 outcomes (winner-take-all / commodity race-to-zero / open-source / **oligopoly**). Of ~60 startups, almost all died; Amazon & others "never showed up." Settled into a **3-horse race: Anthropic (enterprise) / OpenAI (consumer) / Google Gemini** — analogous to the 3-cloud structure underpinning all of SaaS.
	- **Differentiation is real**: routers make models *look* like a commodity, but there's critical IP and skill specialization — **Anthropic strong in PE/finance & code; Google strong at ingesting PDFs**. Open-source (incl. China) can approach the frontier but lacks compute to leapfrog; the 80%→85% benchmark jump is a huge unlock and the open models falter there.
	- **The harness moat**: Anthropic isn't just an API/model — it's building an **ecosystem around the API** (SDK, Claude for Cowork, orchestration, the "harness" software that extracts the most from the model). Analogy: AWS in 2013 — looked like commodity servers, became a product ecosystem with deep lock-in.
	- **Recursive improvement**: Anthropic leads in code → feeds code capability back into the model → innovation pace **accelerating** (potential liftoff / escape velocity).

- ## Coding = "the true unlock of AI"
	- Progression: **gen-1 = Microsoft Copilot (~$20/mo)** (grammar of coding, find a bug, write a paragraph of code) → **mid-2025 Claude Code went agentic** → coding market "exploded."
	- Anthropic engineers reportedly spending **$100/day** on tokens → **~$20–30K/yr/coder**.
	- **TAM math**: ~**20M coders worldwide** × heavy token spend → **~$500B market from coding alone** (on 7–9-month-old tech).
	- **Karpathy / Torvalds flip-flop**: last-gen tools wrote ~20% of code (80% handwritten); after the latest model, Karpathy "hasn't written a line of code except in English." Plus the unlock for people who never knew how to code.

- ## S-Curve Mechanics (the core IP)
	- **Inflection = barriers to adoption removed** → "tornado of demand." Each new platform builds on the prior (Internet 1.0 → mobile → cloud → e-commerce → **AI, the biggest**).
	- **Long flatline before takeoff** (technology exists years before it ignites):
	| Technology | Existed before inflection | Inflection trigger |
	|---|---|---|
	| Smartphone | ~10 yrs before iPhone | Price $500–600 → **$200**, 3G, touchscreen, ecosystem |
	| Internet | ~20 yrs before Netscape | Browser |
	| Electric vehicles | ~15 yrs before 2019 | Price → **$40K**, range → **300 mi**, supply chain |
	| AI | hidden inside companies | ChatGPT (Nov 2022) |
	- **Buy cheap on the S-curve** (the market refuses to think exponentially / >1yr out):
	| Buy | Year | Forward multiple paid |
	|---|---|---|
	| Nvidia | 2023 | **4× forward earnings** |
	| Tesla (car S-curve) | 2019 | **5× earnings** |
	| Apple (early) | — | **4× earnings** |
	| Amazon (for AWS) | 2013 | **"basically free"** |
	- **Height & slope matter** (when to sell / how long to hold):
		- Exponential growth fades at **~30–40% penetration** → sell-side catches up, beats stop. Sacerdote prefers the high-growth **0–50%** zone.
		- **EV warning**: expected 40–50% of cars electric, **stalled at 10–15%** — S-curves usually run to completion but not always; stay adaptive.
		- **Apple sold 2012** at ~50% US smartphone penetration (later compounded ~20% via App Store / ancillaries, but the big years were 0–50%).
	- **Slope varies by integration burden** (Horace Dediu / Clayton Christensen historical S-curve study):
	| Adoption speed | Example | Why |
	|---|---|---|
	| Fastest (~7 yrs to ~100%) | **Radio** | No backend dependency |
	| Slow | **Dishwasher** | Needs backend plumbing |
	| Slow | **B2B / SaaS** | Must integrate into existing systems; security-conscious; needs evangelists |
	| Fast | **Consumer / standalone AI tools** | "Open a browser and it's there" → the vertical L-curve |
	- **Recognizing inflection pre-data**: Andy Grove — "at strategic inflection points you can't trust the data" → intuition + anecdote. Visual/consumer cues (kid in China with a big phone playing a real game) vs hard-to-see enterprise → use proxies like **standing-room-only at Gartner IT Symposium** (Splunk, VMware, AWS ballrooms packed). Peter Lynch: "white out the chart — it's all about the future." **It's OK to be late / miss the first 100%.**

- ## Software / Legacy SaaS — from 40–50% of book to NET SHORT
	- Was **40–50% of portfolio** 5 yrs ago; **sold ~all entering 2026, went net short** (big Q1 help). Initial 2023 thesis (incumbents have salesforces + data + can wrap APIs) failed — **AI products weren't good, didn't move the needle, couldn't charge.**
	- **Four pressures on legacy SaaS**:
		1. AI may be directly disruptive (new software = jet engine vs pen-and-paper).
		2. **Budget/priority displacement** — CIO spends on Anthropic tokens (faster ROI), squeezing software budgets.
		3. **Pricing power gone** — annual price hikes now risky.
		4. **Seat-based erosion** — hiring freezes / job cuts hit seat models.
	- **New "Rule of 40" for AI**: not growth + margin, but **(% of sales that is AI) + (market share in that AI category)**. Legacy SaaS scores poorly — e.g. **Salesforce: ~$40B legacy sales vs ~$500–700M AI ARR** (1–2%).
	- **Half-baked bull twist**: AI may *entrench* network-based systems of record. Agents plug into **Slack** first; CRM/Workday going "headless" → UI disappears but the **database layer solidifies** as agents work directly inside it. Disintermediation of the UI, entrenchment of the data.

- ## Hardware De-Commoditization — "the renaissance of chips"
	- **40 years static**: Intel x86 standard since the '90s; cloud-era compute grew 25–40%/yr but **Moore's Law matched it** → no architectural innovation; everything commoditized (networking went 1→10 Gb on a **~7-yr** cycle).
	- **AI breaks it**: workloads grow **~10× per year**, pushing every component to physical limits → **de-commoditization** (volume growth **+** mandatory architectural innovation at every layer). Shaun Maguire (Sequoia): "I wish I could be a hardware hedge fund."
	- **Component-level winners**:
	| Component | Company | Key facts |
	|---|---|---|
	| Rack assembly / Ethernet switch | **Celestica** | Noticed at **8× earnings**; sole supplier of **Google TPU rack assemblies**; **50–60% share** of cloud Ethernet switch market; strong at **liquid cooling** (rivals failed); co-wrote open-source **SONiC**, close w/ Broadcom |
	| PCB / copper-clad laminate | **Elite Material** | AI server = **40 layers** vs **10** for a regular server; very few global suppliers; **~50–60% CAGR** in component units + rising ASPs/margins |
	| Fiber optics | **Corning** | Microsoft DC has fiber to circle the world **4.5×**; thinner/bendable/spec'd, highest-margin & fastest-growing segment; **scale-up copper→optical = +2–3× TAM** |
	| Power delivery | **Delta Electronics, Advanced Energy** | Each new Nvidia chip/rack uses **+50–125% power** → ASPs +~40%/yr for 4 yrs, higher margin |
	- **Economics shift**: AI rack = **$200–300K** liquid-cooled critical machinery (legacy server **$5K**, disposable). If an AI rack breaks the **whole cluster goes down** → supplier becomes "a critical part on an airplane," never swapped → **5% low-margin grower → 35–50% top-line CAGR w/ rising margins + 4-yr visibility.**
	- **Networking layers**: scale-out (rack-to-rack), **scale-across** (DC-to-DC, needs ~10× thicker wire when power can't co-locate for training), **scale-up** (every GPU in the rack — copper today, **optical later** = the Corning kicker).
	- **Supply is short**: **DRAM, NAND, PCB all ~30% short today** — even a "commodity cycle" would be a great one.
	- **Rate of change > absolute level**: as AI exposure goes 10%→30%, **growth *and* operating margins accelerate** — the lead indicator. (When asked to plot market-share data, Claude missed exactly this rate-of-change dynamic.)

- ## Application Layer — deliberately underweight (for now)
	- "Apps come later" (iPhone breakout apps took years). Risk: **where does model capability end and app workflow begin?** Can apps build a moat vs the model layer? No clear enterprise-app breakout yet; ecosystem still "unclear and a little dangerous."
	- Watching **Brett Taylor's Sierra** (customer-service agents; ex-co-CEO Salesforce, wrote Google Maps, ex-CTO Facebook) as the test of whether a durable app-layer enterprise company can be built. Great app companies *will* come — just not in years 1–4.

- ## Key Risks (what worries the bull)
	- **Public/political sentiment**: only **~20% optimistic** on AI; **Maine banned new data centers** → regulatory risk (though "genie is out of the bottle globally").
	- **Model stagnation**: if frontier labs hit a wall, **open source catches up → race to the bottom** (bad for model stocks; *neutral-to-good for chips* — Jensen *wants* open source to take off; "if good enough is good enough, I won't have a business").
	- **Hyperscaler falters**: one losing the race could strand compute (Oracle canceled → Meta stepped in; risk is if a Meta-scale buyer exits AI entirely). Mitigant: more entrants, even Microsoft building custom silicon.
