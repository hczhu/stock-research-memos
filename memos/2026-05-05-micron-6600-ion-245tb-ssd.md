- ## Micron 6600 ION 245TB SSD — World's Highest-Capacity SSD Now Shipping
	- **Source**: Micron press release, May 5 2026 (Boise). All facts from the release.
	- **Thesis**: Micron is shipping the **245TB Micron 6600 ION**, the world's highest-capacity commercially available SSD, built on **G9 QLC NAND** ("at least one generation ahead of any competing QLC" in data-center SSDs). The pitch is rack-level TCO and energy efficiency vs HDDs as **power becomes the defining constraint for AI infrastructure** — accelerating the HDD→SSD share shift for AI data lakes and object storage.

	| Rack space for equal capacity | 1× | **5.6× more** |

- ## SSD vs HDD Market Share — Bits vs Dollars (industry estimates, web search)
	- **Note**: figures vary by source and definition (total vs enterprise vs data-center); treat as approximate.

	| Metric (≈2026E) | HDD | SSD |
	|---|---:|---:|
	| Capacity shipped — exabytes ("bits") | ~3,200 EB (**~68%**) | ~1,400 EB (**~32%**) |
	| Enterprise/data-center revenue (\$) | ~\$53B | ~\$47–49B (**≈ parity**) |
	| Forward revenue CAGR | ~**−1%** | ~**+8%** |

	- **The key contrast**: HDD still ships ~2/3 of the *bits*, but SSD is roughly **at dollar parity** — i.e., flash earns far more revenue per bit, and the value is migrating to SSD even though HDD wins on raw capacity
	- **Installed base skews further to HDD**: IDC estimates **~89% of data stored by lead cloud providers still resides on HDD** — a large pool still available to convert
	- **Cost crossover**: industry roadmaps point to **enterprise SSD ≈ 10–14TB HDD cost parity around 2026** — the tipping point that widens flash's addressable market (and the backdrop for a 245TB drive)
	- Earlier benchmark: Gartner projected SSDs would reach ~35% of HDD+SSD exabytes shipped by 2026

- ## Key Arguments / Insights
	- **Power is the binding constraint**: "as power availability becomes a defining constraint for AI infrastructure scale," capacity-per-rack and rack-level TCO become the lever — SSD density wins even where HDD is cheaper per raw TB (Jeremy Werner, Micron Core Data Center BU GM)
	- **Storage economics shifting from drive-level to rack-level** (IDC's Jeff Janukowicz): operators need more usable capacity per rack within strict power/cooling limits
	- **AI is pulling storage share from HDD → SSD**: shared-data growth from AI workloads (data lakes, object storage) is the driver
	- **Ecosystem validation**: Dell (Travis Vigil, ISG) integrating 245TB drives into PowerEdge/storage systems for AI, citing "meaningful reduction in total cost of ownership"

- ## Investment Implications ($MU)
	- **Enterprise/data-center SSD is a differentiated growth vertical beyond DRAM/HBM**: Micron claims a ≥1-generation QLC lead — high-capacity QLC is a margin-accretive, leadership product, diversifying Micron's AI exposure past the HBM race
	- **NAND as an inference-era beneficiary**: AI data lakes + object storage + "dump-to-disk" inference workflows lift enterprise NAND demand — corroborates the "NAND is now inference-critical" leg of the memory super-cycle ([[2026-06-01-memory-super-cycle-five-arguments]], [[2026-06-04-memory-market-and-big3-financials]])
	- **HDD substitution risk for Seagate/WDC at the high-capacity tier**: the entire pitch is an explicit HDD-displacement case on density/power/TCO — bearish read-through for HDD at AI/object-storage workloads, even as HDD retains a raw \$/TB edge (cf. [[2026-06-03-memory-stock-exposure-investment-implications]], which flagged HDD "stronger for longer")
	- **Power-constrained AI datacenters structurally favor SSD density**: ties storage into the same power-bottleneck theme as compute/memory ([[2026-06-04-meta-tent-data-centers-btm-capacity]])
	- **Caveat**: vendor-published, best-case lab benchmarks vs HDD arrays; raw \$/TB economics (where HDD still leads) and adoption pace at hyperscalers are the real swing factors — the density/power case is strongest precisely where power is the binding limit
