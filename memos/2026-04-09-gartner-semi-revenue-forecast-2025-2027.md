- ## Gartner Semiconductor Revenue Forecast, Worldwide 2025–2027
	- **Source**: Gartner (April 2026), via @lithos_graphein on X.com (posted 2026-04-09 12:52)
	- **Headline**: Global semi revenue nearly doubles in 2 years to $1.5T — 2026 +64%, 2027 +18%
	- The 2026 surge is almost entirely memory-driven: memory revenue triples (+193% YoY) while nonmemory grows a modest +17%

- ## Context
	- The 2026 jump is almost entirely a memory story: HBM and server DRAM pricing surged as AI capex drove demand far ahead of supply
	- Nonmemory (logic, analog, discretes) growth is steady but unremarkable at ~17% — the headline +64% overstates the breadth of the cycle
	- 2027 growth normalizes to +18% across both segments, suggesting the market expects price/supply to partially equilibrate — but not collapse
	- Memory at $748B in 2027 would still be ~3.5x the 2025 base, implying sustained structural repricing rather than a one-year spike and reversal
	- This forecast likely undercounts upside if AI capex continues accelerating or if memory content per accelerator keeps rising (see Michael Dell's 625x memory demand thesis)
