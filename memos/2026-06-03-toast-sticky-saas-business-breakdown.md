- ## Revenue Model
- **Payments (~2/3 of gross profit)**
	- Net take rate: **~49 bps** on GMV (vs. 75–125 bps for most competitors, ~100 bps for Square)
	- On a $100 dinner: 3% interchange → $2.50 to banks/networks → $0.49 to Toast
	- Take rate has been moving up; meaningfully undermonetized vs. market
	- At $200B GMV and growing, Toast can negotiate better interchange rates at scale
	- Consumption-based, grows with restaurant revenue, inflation, GDP
- **Software (~1/3 of gross profit)**
	- $300–$500/month per customer for the software suite
	- Good/better/best packaging tiers
	- Combined with payments: customer pays ~$10K/year on ~$1.3M revenue = <1% all-in cost
- **Hardware**: loss leader; purpose-built, water/heat/drop-resistant; kitchen display systems, Toast Go handhelds, payments terminal — every customer required to use Toast hardware
- **Lending**: small, profitable; helps restaurants grow capital; nice addition, not core

- ## What Toast Actually Sells
- Full restaurant operating system (described as "Shopify for restaurants"):
	- Point of sale, digital ordering, payroll, automated inventory management, real-time multi-location reporting, marketplace integrations (Uber Eats, etc.)
	- Hardware: kitchen display, handhelds, front-of-house terminals
- **Mission-critical**: if customers turn off Toast, they can't run their business
- Multi-tenant cloud SaaS: code-once, deploy-everywhere with real-time updates → massive advantage over on-premise competitors in AI era (new model every week = competitive moat widens)
- **Toast customers have ~15% operating margins vs. ~10% industry average** — Toast restaurants are more profitable businesses, which also means they survive longer → lower churn for Toast

- ## AI Products — Most Significant Development Since Founding
- ### Toast IQ (conversational AI + system of action)
- Natural language interface to manage the restaurant: ask questions, make menu changes across all locations in real time (used to take a whole weekend)
- Real-time analytics and custom analytics across multiple locations
- Automated inventory management: if produce is running low, system automatically orders from Instacart Business or local vendors
- **50% of customers actively using Toast IQ on a weekly basis**
- Agentic in nature — not just Q&A, but takes actions
- ### Toast Grow (automated marketing engine, newest product)
- $500/month SaaS module
- Analyzes historical data + local restaurant data to predict slow periods, then automatically executes: SMS promos to past visitors, website deals, Instagram promotions
- **8% revenue uplift for customers in early results**
- On avg restaurant with $1.3M revenue: 8% uplift ≈ $104K incremental revenue
- Replaces $1,000–$2,000/month marketing agency spend that produced minimal measurable uplift
- **ROI: ~20x** ($500/month cost vs. ~$104K revenue uplift + $12–24K marketing cost replacement)
- Demonstrates why AI makes Toast's customer ARPU path more durable: new modules are high-value add-ons, not pure pricing extraction
- ### AI as internal R&D leverage
- R&D expense essentially flat in dollar terms over 2 years while company doubled — AI-driven engineering productivity
- "Shipped more product in last two years than ever before"
- Now re-investing in R&D to continue accelerating roadmap

- ## Competitive Landscape
  
  | Competitor | Market share | Assessment |
  |-----------|-------------|------------|
  | Legacy (NCR, Oracle Micros, Loha) | ~50% of market | On-premise, servers in kitchens, can't update real-time → structurally disadvantaged in AI era |
  | Clover (Fiserv) | ~15% | Losing share; Fiserv overlevered post-acquisitions, underinvesting in product |
  | Square | ~5–10% | Lower-end, less feature-rich; winning from legacy/Clover, not from Toast; take rate ~1% GP |
  | DoorDash | Pilot | Piloting POS but unit economics non-rational for restaurants to switch |
- ### Why DoorDash POS Is Not a Real Threat
- Switching from Toast to DoorDash would save restaurant ~50 bps on payments but subject them to 13–15% delivery take rate (vs. Toast's free delivery module via Uber Eats partnership)
- Toast ordering module: ~80,000 restaurants (half the base) already signed up; a $40 DoorDash order costs $30–35 on Toast's platform
- Counter Global channel check: colleague visited 30–40 San Francisco restaurants using both DoorDash and Toast; **zero said they would switch even if DoorDash offered free POS**
- DoorDash better positioned for delivery-first QSR; Toast dominates full-service neighborhood restaurants
- ### Why the Moat Is Hard to Replicate
- Purpose-built hardware: years to design + supply chain + chips + inventory management for share gains
- Distribution: feet on street in every major US city (in-restaurant advocates, relationship-driven sales)
- Data advantage: 160,000 restaurants, local-level transaction data, multi-tenant real-time visibility
- High-profile AI-native startup tried to replicate 1–2 years ago; as of today, not visible in market; roadmap extended at least 2 more years
- Network effects: cities hit flywheel at ~10% market share; some cities now at 25–30% and adding share faster than lower-share cities

- ## Industry Churn — A Feature, Not a Bug (for the Challenger)
- Restaurant industry churns ~15%/year: ~100–120K locations close and reopen annually out of ~800M US restaurants
- In high-retention industries (99% retention), a challenger with a better product gets only 1% at-bats per year
- Toast gets ~100K+ at-bats annually and is **winning ~50% of new openings**
- As Toast gets bigger (30–40% share), will churn increase? So far no noticeable change:
	- Toast restaurants are more profitable (15% vs. 10% margins) → survive longer → lower organic churn
	- Survivorship bias: Toast customers are the healthier cohort

- ## Valuation Framework
- ### Near-term: GAAP PE
- **18x 2027 GAAP PE** for 25%+ gross profit / 30%+ EPS compounder
- Adjusting for ~$2B cash: even lower effective multiple
- Comparable to SaaS multiples that collapsed in 2015 when AWS democratized open source; category killers recovered and "went parabolic" within 18 months
- ### Long-term: MOIC / Intrinsic Value
- Take gross profit from $2B → $10B+ over 10 years → $3B+ GAAP net income
- Will have generated entire current market cap in cumulative cash
- At "horrible" terminal multiple in 2035: **~4x MOIC**
- At NASDAQ market multiple in 2035: **~10x MOIC**
- DCF intrinsic value today: **~$50/share** vs. stock at ~$22–23 → ~2x upside to fair value before long-term compounding
- ### Historical analog
- 2015: AWS open-sourced software → public SaaS multiples collapsed to 3–4x revenue → everyone asked "why pay for software?" → category killers kept putting up numbers → stocks went parabolic in early 2016
- Same movie today with AI disruption narrative; toast is the category killer that keeps winning

- ## Risks
  
  1. **Macro / payments cyclicality**: payments gross profit tracks restaurant volume; 2008–09 restaurant industry was down low single digits — more resilient than feared given people still eat out
  2. **Industry churn dynamics**: as Toast grows to 30–40%+ share, churn could normalize toward industry rate — not yet observed but to monitor
  3. **Customer price sensitivity**: mitigated by ROI-driven product expansion (Toast Grow 20x ROI) vs. pure pricing; still a risk if AI-native modules don't deliver
  4. **Competition**: DoorDash (unit economics non-rational to switch); Square (innovating faster than before); monitoring closely with ongoing customer surveys + channel checks
  5. **SaaS multiple compression**: already happened — 3–4x revenue is current reality; this is the opportunity, not the risk, per the 2015 analog
