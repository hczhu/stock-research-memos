- ## TSMC AGM 2026 — CC Wei: AI Chip Demand Cannot Be Met for Years
	- **Source**: 财联社 (Cailian Press), June 4, 2026; TSMC Annual Shareholder Meeting
	- **Thesis**: TSMC's CEO delivered the clearest supply-constraint statement yet — global AI-driven chip demand will outpace TSMC's supply capacity for years, and even full US production cannot meet US customer needs within any near-term timeframe

- ## AI Demand and Speed of Growth
	- CC Wei: The speed at which AI has grown "caught everyone off guard" — even Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang "didn't know" it would happen this fast
	- Rapid growth made capacity planning difficult; TSMC is "expanding as fast as it can" and "feels the pressure of falling short"
	- AI adoption is spreading across consumer, enterprise, and sovereign AI applications
	- "We have strong confidence in the next few years" — reiterated >30% full-year revenue growth guidance

- ## Supply Constraint Statements
	- **Global supply**: TSMC's worldwide chip supply "cannot meet AI-driven demand for years" — CC Wei's strongest public statement yet on the duration of the structural shortage
	- **US supply**: Fully meeting US customer demand via US-based production would take "a very long time" — no specific timeframe given
	- TSMC is not pursuing memory-style price hikes despite tight supply; committed to "long-term sustainable growth" pricing discipline

- ## Memory Chip Shortage Warning
	- CC Wei said he "would like to" raise prices but wants to avoid aggressive price increases like those seen in memory chips — "that's not sustainable"
	- **Warning on shortage spreading**: Memory chip supply constraints will hit higher-end devices by end of 2026; lower-cost devices already affected — the shortage is now spreading across the entire market
	- This is an external confirmation from the foundry side: memory shortage is broadening, not easing

- ## Competition — TeraFab and Others
	- Wei said competition in semiconductors has "always been fierce" — he wishes Elon Musk well in the TeraFab endeavor
	- TSMC's answer to all competitors (Intel, Samsung, TeraFab) is to "work relentlessly to beat them" through technology leadership and manufacturing efficiency

- ## High-NA EUV (ASML)
	- TSMC has purchased ASML's next-generation High-NA EUV lithography equipment
	- Currently researching how to run the machines more efficiently to lower manufacturing costs
	- "It simply has not been deployed for mass production yet" — Wei called the machines "very expensive"
	- Once they can be used profitably in mass production, TSMC will move forward

- ## TSMC Arizona
	- Will take "a very long time" to fully satisfy US customer needs from US-based fabs — no near-term resolution to geographic concentration risk

- ## TSMC Taiwan
	- TSMC accounts for ~25% of Taiwan's total tax revenue; uses large amounts of land, water, and nearly 10% of the nation's electricity
	- Wei lamented Taiwan's low birth rate — long-term risk of engineer and technician shortage
	- Hopes to promote more family-friendly policies within TSMC to encourage employees to have more children
	- **Employee profit sharing will rise 30% in 2026**; no ceiling on annual profit sharing — signals strong internal confidence in earnings trajectory

- ## Advanced Packaging — CoPoS
	- TSMC has established a pilot line for **CoPoS** (Co-Package Optics with Silicon) advanced packaging
	- "It will take roughly 2–3 years before volumes really get big"
	- CoPoS is a longer-cycle technology ramp than CoWoS — not a near-term revenue driver but confirms TSMC is investing in the next packaging generation

- ## Demand Signal
	- CC Wei: "We continuously see AI model adoption rates rising across consumer, enterprise, and sovereign AI applications… this trend is driving demand for greater compute, which supports strong demand for advanced semiconductors"
	- Customers and their downstream customers — especially cloud service providers — remain optimistic about AI despite rising component costs
	- Advanced nodes contributed 74% of wafer revenue in 2025, up from prior periods — mix shift accelerating toward leading edge

- ## Next Growth Drivers
	- **Autonomous driving**: Identified as a long-term incremental demand driver for TSMC chips
	- **Humanoid robotics**: CC Wei flagged robotics as a priority growth vertical; "semiconductors will always be foundational to these innovations"
	- Both represent new demand waves beyond AI training/inference that are not yet fully in consensus models

- ## Supplier Relations — Tokyo Electron
	- Despite a high-profile 2nm technology theft case involving a Tokyo Electron engineer, Wei confirmed TSMC will continue working with Tokyo Electron — an "excellent supplier"
	- Signal: TSMC prioritizes supply chain continuity over punitive supplier exclusion when the supplier is critical

- ## Investment Implications
	- **Duration of shortage confirmed from the supply side**: CC Wei's "years" language is the strongest foundry-level confirmation of the HBM/AI chip scarcity thesis — aligns with SK Hynix's "at least 2028" projection
	- **Memory shortage broadening is a direct CC Wei warning**: Higher-end devices will be hit by end of 2026 — supports the chipflation thesis spreading beyond the current AI server / low-end device bifurcation
	- **Pricing discipline is a feature, not a bug**: TSMC refusing to raise prices like memory suppliers preserves customer relationships and long-term share — but means TSMC is not the primary chipflation beneficiary; memory makers capture ASP upside
	- **>30% revenue growth in 2026 despite macro uncertainty**: With advanced nodes at 74% of wafer revenue and AI demand accelerating, the growth trajectory is structurally supported
	- **US fab timeline risk**: "Very long time" to serve US demand domestically is a de facto acknowledgment that geographic supply diversification will not resolve near-term AI chip shortages — Taiwan concentration risk remains
	- **High-NA EUV not yet in production**: Positive for ASML order trajectory but negative for near-term cost reduction at TSMC — expensive machines not yet earning their keep
	- **Robotics/autonomy as Act II**: AI inference was Act I; autonomous driving and humanoid robotics represent the next TAM expansion for leading-edge foundry capacity
	- **CoPoS is 2–3 years out**: Not a near-term catalyst; confirms TSMC is building toward the next packaging inflection but it won't be a revenue driver until ~2028–2029
	- **Employee profit sharing +30% three years running**: Signals strong internal confidence in earnings trajectory; unusual to guide this publicly
