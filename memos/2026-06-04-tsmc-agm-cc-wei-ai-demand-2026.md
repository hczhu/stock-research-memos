- ## TSMC AGM 2026 — CC Wei: AI Chip Demand Cannot Be Met for Years
	- **Source**: 财联社 (Cailian Press), June 4, 2026; TSMC Annual Shareholder Meeting
	- **Thesis**: TSMC's CEO delivered the clearest supply-constraint statement yet — global AI-driven chip demand will outpace TSMC's supply capacity for years, and even full US production cannot meet US customer needs within any near-term timeframe

- ## Supply Constraint Statements
	- **Global supply**: TSMC's worldwide chip supply "cannot meet AI-driven demand for years" — CC Wei's strongest public statement yet on the duration of the structural shortage
	- **US supply**: Fully meeting US customer demand via US-based production would take "a very long time" — no specific timeframe given
	- TSMC is not pursuing memory-style price hikes despite tight supply; committed to "long-term sustainable growth" pricing discipline

- ## Demand Signal
	- CC Wei: "We continuously see AI model adoption rates rising across consumer, enterprise, and sovereign AI applications… this trend is driving demand for greater compute, which supports strong demand for advanced semiconductors"
	- Customers and their downstream customers — especially cloud service providers — remain optimistic about AI despite rising component costs
	- Advanced nodes contributed 74% of wafer revenue in 2025, up from prior periods — mix shift accelerating toward leading edge

- ## Next Growth Drivers
	- **Autonomous driving**: Identified as a long-term incremental demand driver for TSMC chips
	- **Humanoid robotics**: CC Wei flagged robotics as a priority growth vertical; "we will work hard to ensure robots can succeed"
	- Both represent new demand waves beyond AI training/inference that are not yet fully in consensus models

- ## Investment Implications
	- **Duration of shortage confirmed from the supply side**: CC Wei's "years" language is the strongest foundry-level confirmation of the HBM/AI chip scarcity thesis — aligns with SK Hynix's "at least 2028" projection
	- **Pricing discipline is a feature, not a bug**: TSMC refusing to raise prices like memory suppliers preserves customer relationships and margins for long-term share — but also means TSMC is not the primary chipflation beneficiary; memory makers capture the ASP upside
	- **>30% revenue growth in 2026 despite macro uncertainty**: With advanced nodes at 74% of wafer revenue and AI demand accelerating, the growth trajectory is structurally supported
	- **US fab timeline risk**: "Very long time" to serve US demand domestically is a de facto acknowledgment that geographic supply diversification will not resolve near-term AI chip shortages — import dependency on Taiwan remains high
	- **Robotics/autonomy as Act II**: AI inference was Act I; autonomous driving and humanoid robotics represent the next TAM expansion for leading-edge foundry capacity — TSMC is positioning to be the primary beneficiary
	- **Employee profit sharing +30% three years running**: Signals strong internal confidence in earnings trajectory; unusual to guide three consecutive years of labor cost growth publicly
