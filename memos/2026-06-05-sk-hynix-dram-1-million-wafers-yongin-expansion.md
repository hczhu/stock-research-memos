- ## SK Hynix to Double DRAM Capacity — ≈1 Million Wafers/Month by 2030–31 (Yongin)
	- **Source**: Korean industry press, reported on the 5th (June 2026); confirmed via SK Hynix partner-company briefings
	- **Thesis**: SK Hynix has told partners it will roughly **double DRAM wafer input — from ~550k to ~1 million wafers/month by 2030–31** — concentrated in the Yongin cluster, with the first equipment move-in pulled forward to **Feb 2027**. NVIDIA's Jensen Huang wrote "**Please make more**" on a Hynix wafer at Computex; SK Group Chairman Chey pledged to "**double total wafer capacity within five years at full speed**." A massive demand-validated supply ramp — and the clearest signal yet that the Big 3 are committing to multi-year DRAM expansion.

- ## Capacity Ramp Plan

	| Metric | Value |
	|---|---|
	| Current DRAM input capacity | ~550,000 wafers/month (incl. Wuxi, China ~200,000) |
	| Target capacity (2030–31) | **~1,000,000 wafers/month** (~2×) |
	| Yongin Phase 1 fab structure | 2 structural frames, **6 cleanrooms** |
	| First equipment move-in | **Feb 2027** (pulled forward from May 2027) |
	| Ramp cadence | +60,000 wafers per cleanroom, a new cleanroom every ~6 months |
	| Yongin Phase 1 total new capacity | **~360,000 wafers/month** by H1 2030 |
	| Cheongju M15X start | H2 2026 at 40,000/month → ~80,000/month in 2027 |
	| New expansion product mix | **All DRAM** (NAND via tech upgrades / more layers, not new wafers) |

- ## The Build-Up Math
	- ~550k current + ~360k (Yongin Phase 1) + ~80k (M15X new) ≈ **~1 million wafers/month by 2030–31**
	- Expansion is **DRAM-only**; NAND capacity grows through layer-count increases rather than added wafer starts — signals where SK Hynix sees the structural demand (AI server DRAM + HBM)
	- This is the **first disclosure of per-cleanroom product/scale and ramp pace**; in February SK Hynix had only revealed the Yongin investment amount and building structure

- ## Demand Validation Signals
	- **Jensen Huang (NVIDIA)** wrote "Please make more" on an SK Hynix DRAM wafer at Computex 2026 — but the expansion plan predates the gesture (partners briefed ~2 months prior)
	- **Chairman Tae Won Chey (SK Group)** at Computex: "we will double total wafer production capacity within five years at full speed" — direct, top-level commitment
	- Chey on pricing discipline: "Prices suddenly jumping or surging can harm overall sustainability… more sustainability is needed" — read as intent to **expand through the cycle without being swayed by short-term price swings** (echoes TSMC CC Wei's anti-"memory-style price hike" stance, [[2026-06-04-tsmc-agm-cc-wei-ai-demand-2026]])

- ## Risks & Skepticism (partner view)
	- **Execution risk on cadence**: filling one cleanroom every 6 months is tight — a single late equipment type could cascade and disrupt the whole schedule
	- **2022 precedent burns trust**: SK Hynix gave partners a capex guideline, then that fall sharply cut order volumes; partners who pre-bought components took direct cash-flow hits. Hence "cautiously watching whether it will be executed"
	- **Demand-contingent**: "The entire plan will only be achieved if market demand backs it up" — the ramp is a plan, not a guarantee
	- **Greater weight than past plans**: the group chairman personally laying out the big picture is read as a stronger commitment signal than prior cycles

- ## Investment Implications
	- **Near-term bullish for WFE / equipment & materials**: A near-doubling of DRAM wafer input = large incremental tool and materials orders starting with Feb 2027 move-in — "a big positive for the equipment and materials industries." Read-through to AMAT, ASML, KLA, Tokyo Electron and Korean equipment/materials suppliers ([[2026-06-04-terafab-wfe-dram-stress-test]], [[2026-04-16-semiconductor-capital-expenditures-2024-2026]]).
	- **Supply-side variable for the super-cycle**: Doubling DRAM capacity by 2030–31 is the kind of aggressive add that, in prior cycles, seeded oversupply when it landed ([[2026-06-02-memory-cycle-bear-case-arguments]]). But the ramp is back-end-loaded (meaningful output ~2030) and DRAM-bit growth is still EUV/packaging-gated near term — so it does little to ease the 2026–28 shortage ([[2026-06-04-memory-market-and-big3-financials]]).
	- **Pricing-discipline framing matters**: Chey's "sustainability over price spikes" language suggests SK Hynix wants durable, LTA-backed expansion rather than a boom-bust spike — consistent with the two-tier/LTA structure ([[2026-06-03-memory-two-tier-market-and-ltas]]). Supportive of mid-cycle margin durability if executed with discipline.
	- **NVIDIA demand pull is explicit**: "Please make more" + Hynix's HBM/server-DRAM leadership reinforce that the incremental DRAM is aimed at AI accelerators — tightening the $NVDA ↔ $000660.KS supply linkage.
	- **Watch the execution tells**: Feb 2027 first move-in, the 6-month cleanroom cadence, and whether SK Hynix holds the plan through any near-term price wobble (vs. the 2022 reversal) are the key milestones to track.
