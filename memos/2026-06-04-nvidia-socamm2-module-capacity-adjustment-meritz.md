- ## NVIDIA NVL72 SoCAMM2 Capacity Cut — Total LPDDR Demand Actually Rising
	- **Source**: Meritz Securities Korea (Sunwoo Kim, Seungsoo Yang), comment on the SoCAMM2 rumor, June 2026
	- **Thesis**: The headline "192GB → 96GB SoCAMM2 capacity cut" is a misread. NVIDIA changed the *mix* (more lower-capacity modules) while raising *total* order volume — net LPDDR demand is up 10–20%. Meritz frames it as a double positive: more near-term module shipments + higher-value substrate/materials longer term.

- ## What Changed — Mix Shift, Not Demand Cut
	- Market concern: LPDDR5X SoCAMM2 module capacity for NVIDIA NVL72 reduced from **192GB → 96GB**
	- Meritz read: noise typical of a DRAM upcycle when stock prices lead; the cycle "has not even reached mid-cycle" — current level is the "thigh," investors should only cut memory exposure at the "shoulder"
	- Per Taiwan/Korea channel checks, NVIDIA changed the **shipment mix** while **increasing total order volume**:

	| Module capacity | Volume change | Role |
	|---|---|---|
	| 192GB | **−50%** (cut in half) | De-emphasized |
	| 96GB | **+~6× (sixfold)** | New mainstream product |
	| 64GB | **+50%** | Increasing |

	- Interpreted as NVIDIA's pragmatic workaround to **prioritize expanding Vera Rubin system shipments amid tight memory supply**
	- **Net result**: total SoCAMM2 LPDDR demand expected to rise **+10–20%** vs prior forecast

- ## Why It's a Positive
	- **Demand ceiling expands, not contracts**: Vera Rubin systems can be **upgraded to higher-capacity modules any time after initial shipment** → lowering initial capacity pulls forward system deployment while preserving (raising) the medium/long-term memory upgrade runway
	- **Volume tailwind for the module value chain**: For SoCAMM2 module, materials, and back-end process suppliers, the shift to higher shipments of lower-capacity products drives a **sharp upward revision to 2026E/2027E shipment volumes**

- ## Value Chain Check
	- Channel checks indicate **no component- or materials-level design change** to SoCAMM2 from the capacity adjustment
		- No major change in substrate specifications, key material composition, or back-end process complexity
		- Therefore the value chain **fully benefits from the higher shipment volume** despite lower capacity per module
	- **Next-gen direction** (medium/long term): expect **higher substrate layer count, more advanced stack structures, and broader low-loss material adoption** to support high-speed signaling and power integrity

- ## Investment Implications
	- **Memory cycle read**: A capacity-cut headline that is actually a demand *increase* is exactly the kind of misunderstanding-driven dip Meritz views as a buying opportunity — consistent with the "still early-cycle" framing (see [[2026-06-01-memory-super-cycle-five-arguments]])
	- **LPDDR / SoCAMM beneficiaries**: Total LPDDR5X demand +10–20% supports DRAM makers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) and the SoCAMM2 module/substrate/back-end supply chain
	- **Substrate & materials**: Double positive — near-term unit volume + next-gen ASP uplift from higher layer counts and low-loss materials; watch substrate suppliers
	- **NVIDIA system cadence**: Lower initial module capacity = a supply-constrained-era tactic to maximize Vera Rubin system shipments now, with field upgrades later — bullish for total system unit deployment
