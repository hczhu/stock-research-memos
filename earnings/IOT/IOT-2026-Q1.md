- **Theme**: Samsara is trying to turn fleet telematics and video safety into a broader physical-operations system of action, with operational AI, maintenance, asset tracking, public sector, and software-only products expanding the platform beyond its original hardware-led beachhead

---

- ## Headline
	- Management framed the quarter around the shift from "bits to atoms": AI moving from software and digital workflows into vehicles, equipment, job sites, infrastructure, and frontline work
	- The core thesis from management:
		- Physical operations customers are scaling because of data center buildout, grid investment, public infrastructure modernization, and private-sector infrastructure demand
		- These customers need real-time visibility across assets, workers, routes, jobsites, safety, compliance, maintenance, and billing
		- Samsara's sensor network creates proprietary physical-world data that can feed AI detections and agents
		- Emerging products are turning Samsara from a point solution into a multi-product operating platform
	- **Read-through**: the important strategic question is whether Samsara can use its installed base of hardware, cameras, vehicle data, and workflows to become the control plane for physical operations rather than remaining a fleet telematics vendor

---

- ## Physical AI Thesis
	- Samsara's strongest narrative is that AI infrastructure buildout creates second-order demand in the physical economy
	- Management highlighted demand from:
		- data center construction
		- power generation
		- energy systems
		- cooling infrastructure
		- grid and transmission capacity
		- public infrastructure modernization
		- private enterprises scaling physical operations
	- The shareholder letter cited McKinsey's estimate that global infrastructure needs will require roughly $106 trillion of investment by 2040
	- Samsara's customers sit in asset-heavy, labor-intensive industries where operations budgets cover labor, equipment, vehicles, maintenance, fuel, accidents, and insurance
	- Management emphasized that the largest customers spend heavily on operating costs, making Samsara's target budget pool closer to core operations than discretionary software
	- **Read-through**: the budget pool is less discretionary than classic enterprise software seats because Samsara is selling into core operating costs, not only office productivity

- ## Products
	- **Connected Asset Maintenance**
		- Helps customers move from time-based and mileage-based maintenance to real-time, data-driven maintenance
		- Management said the market is still early because many sophisticated fleets know they are over-maintaining or under-maintaining assets but historically lacked the integrated data to act on that insight
		- The product combines fault-code intelligence, vehicle diagnostics, work orders, preventive maintenance, warranty, inventory, vendor workflows, and technician experience
		- The product is strategically important because every asset-heavy customer has a maintenance problem, and maintenance is close to the budget owner
	- **Asset Tags**
		- Extends Samsara's visibility beyond plugged-in vehicles to smaller tools, equipment, trailers, field assets, and assets where traditional hardware was not viable
		- Management described another strong quarter for Asset Tags, with contribution coming broadly across emerging products rather than from one narrow SKU
	- **AI Video-Based Safety / AI Multicam**
		- Continues to serve as a core beachhead, especially for large fleets and public-sector users
		- Cameras are increasingly treated as sensors that can feed new operational AI products, not just as evidence tools for driver safety
	- **Connected Workflows and Commercial Navigation**
		- Management used a food-service distributor example to show Samsara expanding from safety into telematics replacement, asset gateways, navigation, and field-work digitization
		- The strategic point is that workflow and navigation products make Samsara closer to day-to-day execution software for frontline operators

---

- ## Operational AI and Agents
	- Management called worker capacity one of customers' biggest constraints on growth
	- Physical operations headcount often scales directly with operating scale, while frontline turnover rates were described as structurally high
	- Many required roles are specialized: electricians, heavy-equipment operators, construction specialists, technicians, and drivers
	- Samsara's operational AI pitch is to use cameras and sensor data to detect real-world conditions, then use agents to automate routine tasks so each worker can accomplish more
	- Initial focus areas:
		- waste management
		- public sector
		- student transportation
	- **Waste Intelligence**
		- Service verification confirms whether collection occurred at the scheduled time and location
		- Overfill detection identifies containers that exceed capacity and can support added billing
		- Contamination detection is being developed to identify non-recyclable or hazardous materials and enforce contamination policies
		- The important commercial angle is monetizing missed service events, not only reducing costs
	- **Ground Intelligence**
		- Uses AI dashcam, multicam, and telematics g-force data to assess pothole type and severity
		- Management said Samsara's data network covers 99% of major U.S. roads
		- The product turns road maintenance from citizen-call and survey-driven work into a continuously updated defect map
		- The important commercial angle is a software/data product that can be sold even without Samsara-owned hardware in the customer fleet
	- **Ridership Management**
		- Part of the public-sector operational AI bundle introduced at Go Beyond
		- Relevant to student transportation and public-sector workflows where visibility, compliance, and route accountability matter
	- **Agentic pricing**
		- Management said some visual intelligence products can be sold as additional SKUs alongside existing products
		- Ground Intelligence can be priced as a per-mile data product
		- For agents, management expects to test consumption-based pricing once it finds the right applications and customer fit
	- **Read-through**: Samsara's AI opportunity is not generic copilots; it is computer vision plus physical telemetry plus workflow automation attached to existing operating budgets

- ## Customers
	- **Global engineering, architecture, and environmental consulting firm**
		- Customer has more than 34,000 employees and operates across energy, transportation, and public works
		- Uses Samsara Telematics across heavy-duty trucks, medium- and light-duty vehicles, and passenger cars in the U.S. and Canada
		- Uses Asset Tags for trailers, marine vessels, ATVs, and field equipment
		- Uses Samsara to track vehicle usage by driver, project, and business versus personal use, supporting project billing and tax reporting
		- Also deploying AI Video-Based Safety for driver behavior detection, in-cab coaching, and safety-score improvement
		- **Read-through**: the customer shows Samsara moving into billing, compliance, asset visibility, and safety workflows inside infrastructure-related operations
	- **Large Canadian supermarket chain**
		- Customer has more than 1,600 stores and 128,000 employees
		- Uses Samsara Connected Asset Maintenance to replace a legacy maintenance system that was disconnected from vehicle data
		- Fault codes and inspection reports automatically trigger work orders
		- Uses preventive maintenance scheduling, dynamic fleet assignment, work orders, vendor management, parts inventory, AI invoice scanning, warranty recovery, and total cost-of-ownership tracking
		- **Read-through**: the example shows why maintenance can become a broad operational system rather than a narrow fleet module
	- **Hertz**
		- Management described Hertz as operating one of the largest vehicle fleets in the world, with roughly half a million vehicles
		- The initial deal is software-only Connected Asset Maintenance across the North American vehicle fleet
		- Hertz has fast vehicle turnover, making maintenance software the most relevant starting point
		- Management said the broader platform still has potential relevance in other parts of Hertz's operations
		- **Read-through**: software-only deals can open very large accounts where hardware deployment is less natural at first, and they avoid some of the hardware cost burden attached to traditional deployments
	- **World's largest food-service distributor**
		- Adopted AI Video-Based Safety in 2018 and has completed many expansions since then
		- Replaced an incumbent telematics provider with Samsara this quarter
		- Added Asset Gateways, Commercial Navigation, and Connected Workflows, becoming a five-product customer
		- **Read-through**: large-customer expansion is often driven by adding more assets and licenses to existing products, but the platform story becomes stronger when customers replace incumbents and add workflow modules
	- **Public sector**
		- Public-sector customers adopt a similar platform pattern to commercial fleets: telematics, cameras, equipment trackers, and Connected Asset Maintenance
		- Samsara has integrations for public-sector reporting systems, with management citing 350 platform integrations overall
		- **Read-through**: public sector looks more like an adjacent vertical than a completely separate product category, which should help product leverage if adoption continues

- ## Go-To-Market
	- Samsara is leaning further into large, complex physical-operations customers
	- Management said large customers have:
		- large frontline workforces
		- tens of thousands of vehicles, trailers, equipment, and assets
		- complex coordination problems across distributed operations
		- greater need for one integrated platform
	- Large deals have longer and less predictable enterprise sales cycles, so management is encouraging investors to look at longer-period net-new demand rather than single-quarter timing
	- Expansion is more often driven by adding more assets or licenses to products already deployed, with cross-selling new products as an additional but smaller driver
	- Many large lands already include multiple products upfront
	- Management is adding direct-sales capacity while also seeing productivity gains from AI and better rep effectiveness
	- **Read-through**: the growth model is not just more logos; it is more enterprise standardization, more assets, more licenses, and more software modules inside the same operating footprint

---

- ## Software-Only Product Structure
	- The Hertz maintenance deal is important because it is software-only
	- Management said software-only products avoid hardware amortization, which is one of Samsara's largest cost-of-goods items
	- Emerging products increasingly include software-only or data-only modules
	- Ground Intelligence is especially interesting because it can be sold as a data product without requiring the customer to deploy Samsara hardware
	- **Read-through**: if more emerging products are software-only overlays on the installed sensor network, Samsara can improve product breadth and potentially reduce dependence on hardware-heavy economics over time

- ## Supply Chain and Competitive Read-Through
	- Management acknowledged tighter DRAM and NAND supply-chain conditions and rising component prices
	- Samsara said visibility is worse than in the past, especially across multi-quarter planning windows
	- Management still said it has generally been able to find enough supply to meet demand
	- Samsara believes its capital position may become a competitive advantage if smaller competitors struggle with supply, price, or lead-time constraints
	- Sanjit said it is still early to know whether this creates a real share-gain window, but the second half of the year may reveal more
	- **Read-through**: hardware supply tightness is both a cost risk and a potential competitive filter; the outcome depends on whether Samsara's supply-chain execution lets it keep selling while weaker vendors stumble

- ## Internal AI Use
	- Samsara said engineering teams have used coding bots and agents for some time
	- Management said AI helps engineering develop more features and go deeper with customers
	- AI is also being used in G&A and go-to-market workflows for account research, customer context, and task automation
	- Headcount growth is expected to be concentrated in go-to-market, while other functions may remain roughly flat or smaller
	- **Read-through**: management is trying to fund more product and sales capacity through AI-driven productivity rather than broad headcount growth across every function

- ## Forward-Looking Data Points
	- Global infrastructure investment need cited at roughly $106 trillion by 2040
	- Large customers have deep operating-cost pools across labor, equipment, vehicles, maintenance, fuel, accidents, and insurance
	- The average age of light-duty vehicles rose from 11.5 years to 12.8 years over the past decade
	- Labor and parts costs for maintenance have risen 27% since 2020
	- Maintenance consumes roughly 10% of operational budgets for physical-operations organizations
	- Frontline worker turnover rates were described as 40% to 50%, making automation and worker productivity strategic rather than cosmetic
	- Potholes cost U.S. drivers about $3 billion annually, while about 39% of major U.S. roads remain in poor or mediocre condition
	- Samsara's vehicle data network covers 99% of major U.S. roads
	- Public-sector reporting is supported by a broader integration ecosystem, with management citing 350 integrations
	- **Read-through**: these data points support a thesis that Samsara's TAM expands when sensors, AI, and workflows attack concrete operating-cost pools: maintenance, labor, insurance, fuel, safety, billing leakage, route efficiency, and infrastructure repair

- ## Read-Through
	- This was a more explicit "physical AI" call than prior Samsara earnings calls
	- The key product evolution is:
		- telematics and cameras instrument the physical world
		- proprietary sensor data creates an AI training and detection advantage
		- operational AI identifies events and conditions
		- agents and workflows automate frontline tasks
		- software-only and data-only products expand monetization beyond hardware deployments
	- The strongest bullish signal is that emerging products are not isolated experiments; maintenance, asset tags, multicam, public-sector intelligence, navigation, and workflows all attach to the same operational data model
	- The strategic risk is execution complexity: Samsara is moving across many end markets and product layers at once, while large deals have inherently uneven timing
	- Competitive risk is also shifting: legacy telematics vendors may lose share if customers standardize on a broader platform, but software-first AI tools could pressure point modules if Samsara does not move fast enough
	- The most important follow-up for future quarters is whether operational AI and software-only products start showing repeatable adoption outside early customer examples
