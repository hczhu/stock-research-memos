- **Source**: Cloudflare Q4 2021 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Zero Trust is accelerating, Workers is emerging as the third act, and the network architecture keeps proving adaptable

---

- ## Market and Product View
	- Management argued that two head fakes slowed cloud migration over the prior four years:
		- The Trump tax cuts pulled hardware CapEx forward
		- COVID caused customers to buy more of what they already had rather than redesign architectures
	- Large revenue contributors, from largest to smallest:
		- CDN / WAF / DDoS / DNS
		- Zero Trust
		- Workers, which had not yet hit its “hockey-stick” phase

---

- ## Metrics and Investments
	- Cloudflare said it had consistently spent about **11% to 12% of revenue** on network traffic, with new guidance of **12% to 14%**
	- **R2** closed beta reached **9,000+ signups**
	- The company ended the year with:
		- **121** customers spending over **$500K**
		- **56** customers spending over **$1M**

---

- ## M&A and Platform
	- Cloudflare acquired **Vectrix**, an API-driven CASB startup
	- Management described Vectrix as a natural add-on to every current Cloudflare customer
	- The company also referenced the prior acquisition of **Zaraz**, which had been built entirely on Workers

---

- ## Customers
	- A **Fortune 500 retailer** moved away from two competitors to adopt **Cloudflare Workers**
	- The company signed a **3-year, $1.2M** contract

---

- ## Read-Through
	- This quarter shows the sequencing of the thesis clearly:
		- Start with network/security
		- Grow into Zero Trust
		- Add Workers as the third act
