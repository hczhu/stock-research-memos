- **Source**: Cloudflare Q4 2020 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Zero Trust, excess-capacity monetization, and global network flexibility are becoming central to the story

---

- ## R&D and Architecture
	- Management said `ETI` represented about **10% of R&D efforts**
	- Every server on the network was designed to run every bit of Cloudflare software
	- This means new products can be layered onto the existing footprint without a proportional increase in cost infrastructure
	- The product team intentionally looks for excess resources on the network and then tries to build products to monetize them

---

- ## Zero Trust
	- Management argued that a **Zero Trust** approach acts like bulkheads in a ship
	- The SolarWinds episode highlighted the weakness of legacy “castle-and-moat” architectures
	- Cloudflare’s argument is that segmentation and verification can prevent attackers from moving laterally once they enter an organization

---

- ## Platform and Cloud Interconnection
	- Cloudflare emphasized its role as the network connecting different clouds and SaaS systems
	- The **Bandwidth Alliance** was presented as a way to lower cloud-data-transfer costs and make cross-cloud traffic more efficient
	- Management argued that customers could gain Cloudflare’s security, access controls, and visibility while also saving on cloud spend

---

- ## Product Examples
	- Cloudflare launched functionality tying employee login/location signals into HR and payroll systems for tax compliance in a remote-work world

---

- ## Read-Through
	- This quarter is important because it explains the architectural and economic logic of the company, not just the headline products
