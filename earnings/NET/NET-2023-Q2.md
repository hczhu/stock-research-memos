- **Source**: Cloudflare Q2 2023 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Net retention softened, but Workers, R2, and large-customer momentum remained strong

---

- ## Go-to-Market and Retention
	- Dollar-based net retention fell to **115%**, down **2 points QoQ**
	- Management said NRR is a **lagging indicator** and will be slower to reflect go-to-market improvements

---

- ## Products and Adoption
	- Cloudflare highlighted four competitive displacement motions:
		- Traditional hardware vendors in application security
		- Point cloud solutions
		- First-generation Zero Trust vendors
		- Traditional object stores, via **R2** and its no-egress economics
	- **Workers** reached **10M active applications** in Q2
		- Up **250%** since December
		- Up **490% YoY**
	- **R2** reached **44,000 distinct paying customers**
	- Cloudflare added a record number of customers spending more than **$500K** annually
	- The company also had one of its strongest quarterly additions of customers spending more than **$1M** annually

---

- ## Customers
	- A **Fortune 500 technology services company** signed a **3-year, $7.2M** Zero Trust deal for **25,000 users**
		- Annual spend rose to over **$5M**
	- One of the largest online recruiting platforms signed a **25-month, $2.4M** expansion and crossed **$5M** annual spend
	- An **Australian technology company** signed a **1-year, $2.2M** expansion and crossed **$5M** total spend

---

- ## Read-Through
	- The quarter is a good example of why Cloudflare is a messy but interesting story: retention pressure on one side, very real product-adoption and upmarket signals on the other
