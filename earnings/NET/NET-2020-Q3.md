- **Source**: Cloudflare Q3 2020 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Strong hiring selectivity, predictable pricing, and early proof that vendor consolidation can drive meaningful enterprise wins

---

- ## Hiring and Culture
	- In Q2 2020 Cloudflare received more than **40,000** job applications and extended offers to **0.6%**
	- In Q3 2020 the company received more than **60,000** job applications and extended offers to **0.4%**

---

- ## Customers and Vendor Consolidation
	- No customer represented more than **5%** of revenue
	- The top 20 customers were well under **20%** of total revenue
	- A **Fortune 500 industrial manufacturing company** replaced multiple vendors and products with Cloudflare, signing a **$1.4M annual contract**
	- A **Fortune 500 food and beverage company** replaced a legacy network optimization vendor and signed a **$500K annual contract**
		- They also cut spend in half versus the previous vendor
	- A **Fortune 500 pharmaceutical company** adopted the **Cloudflare One** architecture
		- Onboarded **47,000 employees and contractors**
		- Protected **130+ applications**
		- Signed a **$1.5M annual contract**

---

- ## Pricing and Customer Experience
	- Cloudflare emphasized fixed and predictable pricing for core services
	- Management’s view was that a security provider should not surprise customers with variable bills during an attack
	- This predictable-pricing argument became more resonant after the volatility of 2020

---

- ## Read-Through
	- The quarter shows an early version of the mature Cloudflare story:
		- better economics than legacy vendors
		- simpler platform
		- good enough breadth to consolidate multiple products
