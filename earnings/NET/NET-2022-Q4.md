- tags:: [[Cloudflare]], [[NET]], [[SaaS]], [[networking]], [[security]], [[AI]], [[earnings]]

- **Source**: Cloudflare Q4 2022 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: GTM weakness is acknowledged, but customer evidence shows strong product breadth in AI, Zero Trust, R2, and public sector

---

- ## Go-to-Market
	- Management admitted the go-to-market organization had **not yet been fully optimized**
	- As products became more complex and the target customer moved upmarket, Cloudflare said it needed to **step up its game in marketing and sales**

---

- ## Customers
	- Cloudflare won a **$7.2M, 5-year** contract to operate the **`.gov` registry**
	- A **leading generative AI company** signed a **1-year, $1M** deal
		- The relationship began as a small gateway DNS opportunity intended to replace Cisco Umbrella
		- Cloudflare then expanded into CDM, DDoS, bot management, gateway DNS, and more as the customer’s prior setup proved insufficient
	- A **Fortune 500 energy company** signed a **3-year, $1.6M** deal replacing a first-generation Zero Trust competitor
	- A **Fortune 500 financial services company** signed a **3-year, $1.1M** expansion
	- A **European financial services company** signed a **5-year, $1.8M** deal replacing about a **dozen** security and network vendors
	- Management said the **largest R2 customer** was another AI company using Cloudflare as a neutral place to store training data

---

- ## Retention
	- Dollar-based net retention was **122%**
		- Down **200 bps QoQ**
		- Down **300 bps YoY**
	- Management said this was driven mainly by **less net expansion** in customers below **$100K** and pay-as-you-go accounts, not by elevated churn

---

- ## Read-Through
	- This quarter reinforces the central thesis: Cloudflare’s lower end may matter less over time if the company can keep replacing higher-value point solutions in the enterprise
