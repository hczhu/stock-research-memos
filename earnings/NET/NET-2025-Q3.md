- **Source**: Cloudflare Q3 2025 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Connectivity cloud framing, enterprise-sales transition, and early AI-native demand signals

---

- ## Strategy
	- Cloudflare described itself as a **connectivity cloud**
	- Management said the company is transforming from a **product-led company** into a **true enterprise sales company**
	- The suite had **55 revenue-contributing products**

---

- ## Products
	- Introduced `NET Dollar`, a U.S.-dollar-backed stablecoin concept fully collateralized 1:1 by USD
	- AI-native companies initially show stronger interest in **security products** than in inference products
	- Management’s logic: when AI queries are expensive, preventing abuse and fraudulent traffic is economically critical

---

- ## Customers
	- Management estimated that roughly **80% of AI companies** use Cloudflare in some way
	- A **leading European tech company** signed a **5-year, $34.3M** extension, including **$6.8M** of upsell, for Workers and application services
		- The customer is redesigning its architecture around Workers and Durable Objects
		- Motivation: avoid egress fees, reduce latency, and escape hyperscaler lock-in
	- A **Fortune 500 fintech company** signed a **2-year, $16.1M pool-of-funds** contract with **$4.6M** of upsell for application services and Workers
	- A **U.S. cabinet-level agency** signed a **2-year contract exceeding $20M** for the full FedRAMP portfolio
	- A **global web infrastructure platform** signed a **14-month, $1.2M** contract for AI crawl control and bot management
		- Cloudflare is exploring a larger **pay-per-crawl** opportunity with this customer

---

- ## Read-Through
	- The quarter supports the thesis that Cloudflare’s enterprise motion is becoming more intentional, not accidental
	- The best AI opportunity may be **protecting and governing AI traffic**, not competing directly with hyperscalers on raw inference
