- **Source**: Snowflake FY2027 Q1 earnings call transcript (`snowflake-2026Q1-earnings-transcript.pdf`)
- **Theme**: AI is compounding Snowflake's core data platform by accelerating migrations, making data work faster, and turning Snowflake Intelligence + Cortex Code into the first surfaces of an enterprise agentic control plane

---

- ## Headline
	- Snowflake framed the quarter as a shift from "AI as a tailwind" to AI becoming a direct product and workflow engine inside Snowflake
	- The core thesis from management:
		- AI accelerates consumption of the core data platform because customers need governed enterprise data to power agents
		- Snowflake Intelligence and Cortex Code are becoming the business-user and builder interfaces for the agentic control plane
		- Native AI products create second-order consumption because prompts become pipelines, agents, apps, and production workflows on Snowflake
	- Management repeatedly described Snowflake as the **agentic control plane** where enterprise intent becomes governed action across data, models, applications, and workflows

---

- ## Products
	- **Snowflake Intelligence**
		- Business-user surface for asking questions, getting answers, and triggering actions on enterprise data
		- Accounts using Snowflake Intelligence more than doubled quarter-over-quarter
		- Management's key claim is that Snowflake Intelligence turns governed data into a conversational action layer, not just a BI interface
	- **Cortex Code / CoCo**
		- Builder interface for creating applications, pipelines, agents, and workflows directly in Snowflake
		- CoCo is a general-purpose coding agent specialized for Snowflake and data-platform work
		- Management said CoCo can outperform frontier models for Snowflake-specific operations
		- CoCo has expanded to support adjacent data tools and platforms, including Amazon Glue, Airflow, dbt Cloud, and Databricks
		- CoCo went GA at the start of the quarter and is already in use with more than 7,100 accounts
		- Management called Snowflake Intelligence and CoCo the fastest-adopted new products in Snowflake's history
	- **Natoma**
		- Intended acquisition extends Snowflake's agentic control plane beyond data and development workflows into everyday applications
		- Examples include sending emails, summarizing Slack conversations, checking calendars, and opening Jira tickets from Snowflake Intelligence or CoCo
		- The important point is governance: actions happen from an environment with enterprise security, permissions, observability, and policy enforcement
	- **Cloud runtime**
		- Public preview product that lets CoCo-style work execute in the cloud in a governed manner
		- Enables autonomous agents because workflows no longer require a local laptop session to remain open
		- Shares infrastructure with Snowflake Intelligence and CoCo: harness, runtime, model garden, and session memory
	- **Model choice**
		- Snowflake emphasized partnerships with leading AI labs while preserving customer choice
		- Cortex Code and Snowflake Intelligence can run across multiple model providers and Snowflake-hosted models
		- Management positioned this neutrality as similar to Snowflake's cloud-provider independence

- ## AI Data Flywheel
	- Snowflake's core argument is that enterprise AI starts with the "Gold layer" of governed data already stored in Snowflake
	- 42% of customers are data sharing on Snowflake with at least one stable edge, reinforcing the platform's role as an inter-company data network
	- Management said Snowflake has strong context for AI because it sees:
		- high-value enterprise data
		- dashboard and application activity built on top of that data
		- metadata and query activity
		- user permissions and governance policies
	- Christian Kleinerman said Snowflake historically used metadata and activity for query optimization and performance; now the same signals are being used to provide better AI context
	- Sridhar described Cortex Code as strategic because repeated use across an enterprise creates memory concepts that make CoCo better for future use
	- **Read-through**: the moat is not just data storage; it is governed data plus metadata, access control, query history, activity context, and repeated agent usage inside the same platform

- ## Go-To-Market
	- Snowflake's new CRO, Jonathan Beaulier, is repositioning the sales organization for the AI era
	- Management said AI is making the go-to-market organization more effective:
		- account executives can learn faster and pitch more relevant products
		- solution engineers can build realistic demos and prototypes much faster
		- CoCo and Snowflake Intelligence make Snowflake easier to demonstrate live to executives
	- Use cases deployed in the quarter increased 114% year-over-year
	- Use cases won per account executive increased 86% year-over-year
	- **Read-through**: AI products are not only products to sell; they are sales accelerants because they shorten the path from customer idea to working prototype

- ## Internal AI Use
	- Snowflake is using CoCo and Snowflake Intelligence internally as proof points
	- Support organization:
		- CoCo analyzes incoming customer cases before an engineer engages
		- It surfaces diagnostic insights and likely root causes upfront
		- Management said this drove over 25% faster case resolution times and a 25% increase in case throughput per engineer
	- Cloud deployment engineering:
		- CoCo freed capacity and shifted resources toward product innovation
		- Management said it reduced complex case resolution time by nearly 30% and cut engineering time spent per ticket by roughly 40%
	- Data organization:
		- CoCo doubled developer productivity as measured by pull requests and lines of code per engineer
		- More than 100 workflows were automated across finance, marketing, sales, and HR in weeks
	- Product velocity:
		- Snowflake delivered over 20% more product capabilities to market than a year ago
	- **Read-through**: Snowflake is using the same tools internally to prove the operating model, not only selling an abstract AI roadmap to customers

- ## Competitive Read-Through
	- Snowflake's answer to AI labs and hyperscalers is neutrality plus governance
	- Management emphasized that enterprises have already configured Snowflake for trusted access to data:
		- data masking
		- role-level policies
		- governance
		- security configuration
		- identity
		- disaster recovery and replication
	- Christian's key point: AI amplifies the value of existing governance rather than forcing customers to rebuild the wheel elsewhere
	- Sridhar said Snowflake is building new controls and policies so governance can be specified more by policy than by tedious SQL mechanics
	- **Read-through**: Snowflake's AI defense is that enterprise-grade governance is hard to recreate, and agents make that governance more important rather than less

- ## Forward-Looking Data Points
	- Snowflake Intelligence and CoCo are the two initial product surfaces for the agentic control plane
	- CoCo adoption is pulling through more core-platform consumption because it makes pipelines, migrations, applications, and agents easier to build
	- Management said migrations that previously took years are now expected to happen over much shorter timelines as CoCo and migration harnesses mature
	- Natoma brings SaaS application context and actions into Snowflake's agent environment through MCP-style connectivity
	- Cloud runtime should let Snowflake host governed autonomous agents that keep running without local developer sessions
	- Management expects agentic development techniques to keep improving quickly, with new methods emerging weekly
	- Snowflake sees the future product architecture as shared infrastructure across business-user agents and builder agents rather than isolated tools

- ## Read-Through
	- This was the clearest Snowflake call so far around a coherent AI strategy:
		- governed enterprise data as the base layer
		- Snowflake Intelligence for business users
		- CoCo for builders
		- Natoma / MCP for application actions
		- cloud runtime for governed autonomous execution
	- The most important insight is that Snowflake is trying to move from data warehouse to enterprise agent operating layer
	- CoCo may be especially important because it both monetizes directly and reduces friction in every workload that would otherwise require data engineering labor
	- The risk is cost governance: management acknowledged that broad rollout of AI tools requires account, agent, and user-level controls on token usage
	- The bullish interpretation is that cost concerns are manageable because customers are comparing AI spend against very large human capital costs in data, analytics, migrations, and workflow automation
