- **Source**: Figma 2026 Q1 earnings call transcript (`figma-2026Q1-earnings-transcript.pdf`)
- **Theme**: Figma is positioning itself as the creative control layer above increasingly cheap code, with Make, MCP, Weave, AI credits, and full-seat upgrades expanding Figma from collaborative design into an AI-native product-building platform

---

- ## Headline
	- Dylan Field framed the AI era as a shift in the software bottleneck:
		- code is becoming more plentiful and easier to write
		- the distance between idea and implementation is collapsing
		- the scarce asset becomes taste, judgment, creativity, craft, and knowing what is worth building
	- Figma's strategic claim is that design becomes more important when execution gets cheaper
	- The company is trying to make Figma the workspace where humans, agents, design systems, code, and direct visual manipulation all meet
	- **Read-through**: Figma is not pitching itself as a legacy design tool with AI features; it is pitching itself as the interface layer for software creation when code generation becomes commoditized

---

- ## Product Direction
	- **Figma Make**
		- Used for tools, prototypes, website designs, and actual shipped software
		- Recent updates let users bring more context into prompts and retain more control over decisions as they build
		- Management said early technical choices constrained Make, but those constraints have been corrected
		- The strategic point is that Make is moving from vibe-coded prototype surface toward a controlled, context-aware product-building surface
	- **MCP**
		- Lets agents read and write directly to Figma files
		- Management said MCP weekly active users in Design grew 5x quarter-over-quarter
		- MCP is strategically important because it connects design files to developer and agent workflows
		- Customers using MCP appear to expand full seats faster than customers not using MCP
	- **Figma Weave**
		- Formerly Weavy
		- Extends Figma into high-fidelity image, video, and visual-generation workflows
		- Management highlighted node-based editing where agents and models work on the same canvas
		- Weave expands Figma beyond product design into marketing, architecture, presentation, and broader creative production
	- **AI Assistant**
		- Currently in alpha
		- Expected to bring AI-native creative workflows directly into the Figma Design canvas
		- Important because management sees more AI consumption surfaces coming beyond Make
	- **Code Connect / code-to-canvas**
		- Management said customers need to reduce the time and token cost of translating between IDEs, terminals, generated code, and design surfaces
		- Figma wants bidirectional workflows: canvas to code and code back to canvas
		- Dylan argued long-term product interfaces around models and inference should become richer than terminals and IDEs

- ## Customer Examples
	- **Google**
		- Longtime Figma customer using Figma end-to-end for AI-native Gemini enterprise experiences
		- Google team described Figma as necessary for reaching a level of detail not possible with vibe coding
		- **Read-through**: Figma's best defense against generic code generation is high-fidelity product detail and the design-system context that large product teams need before shipping
	- **Lufthansa**
		- Used Figma Make to prototype Lido mPilot, an integrated iOS charting app for commercial pilots
		- Connected Make to an in-house API so pilots could test dynamic map gestures before production code
		- **Read-through**: Make is useful when prototypes need real data, real interactions, and stakeholder validation before engineering implementation
	- **Rocket Mortgage**
		- Built its design system directly into Figma Make as shared template infrastructure
		- Adoption expanded beyond design into dashboards, presentations, and customer-experience exploration
		- **Read-through**: enterprise design systems may become AI prompt infrastructure, not just component libraries
	- **NBBJ**
		- Architecture firm using Figma Weave to generate photorealistic renderings from 3D models
		- Replaces slower external rendering workflows with in-house iteration over lighting, urban context, materiality, and human-scale figures
		- Management said Weave adoption at NBBJ is expected to triple in the next few months
		- **Read-through**: Weave broadens Figma's TAM into creative workflows where precision, iteration speed, and client persuasion matter
	- **Large hyperscaler**
		- Unified fragmented Figma usage across thousands of product and engineering teams into a single enterprise agreement
		- **Read-through**: Figma can still consolidate bottom-up adoption into large enterprise standardization even inside the most technically sophisticated buyers
	- **Global media and entertainment company**
		- Started with organic Make usage and moved to a broader rollout
		- As teams adopted Make, developers upgraded to full seats
		- **Read-through**: Make can pull developers into Figma as creators, not only as handoff reviewers
	- **Industrial automation company in Europe**
		- Engineers now outnumber designers on Figma
		- The team is leaning into MCP to connect design directly into the development environment
		- **Read-through**: Figma's user base may increasingly skew toward cross-functional product teams rather than pure designers

- ## AI Monetization
	- Figma began enforcing AI credit limits during the quarter
	- Management described early credit behavior as ahead of expectations
	- The model is deliberately hybrid:
		- full-seat upgrades for users who need more AI capability, Make access, or higher MCP rate limits
		- AI credit add-ons for incremental consumption
		- future usage-based offerings around MCP and agentic workflows
	- Management said customers want governance and control over which teams receive credits and how credits are consumed
	- New monetization infrastructure includes:
		- expanded admin controls
		- pay-as-you-go for Pro customers
		- more flexible enterprise contracting
	- **Read-through**: Figma is trying to make AI monetization support adoption rather than become a hard cap on experimentation; the best outcome is both more seats and more consumption

---

- ## AI Cost Structure
	- Management acknowledged that deeper AI usage creates inference cost pressure
	- The company is focused on optimizing gross profit dollars while pushing growth and usage
	- Cost levers include:
		- routing queries across models based on task complexity
		- using model-agnostic architecture to optimize across providers
		- investing in first-party models trained on Figma's design corpus
		- encouraging users to choose the right tool for the task rather than using inference for everything
	- Dylan said some direct-editing workflows in Figma Design can be much faster than AI, while some bulk or generative workflows are much faster with AI
	- **Read-through**: Figma's UX challenge is also a cost challenge: the product needs to route users between direct manipulation and inference in ways that preserve creativity while avoiding wasteful token burn

---

- ## Competitive Read-Through
	- Analysts asked directly about design tools from hyperscalers and frontier labs
	- Dylan said the landscape includes both complementors and direct competitors
	- Google was framed as a major partner, with Gemini viewed positively and Google's labs product not showing a substantial negative effect in Q1
	- Anthropic was treated as strategically serious because frontier labs can train first-party models and pair them with their own products
	- Figma's cited differentiators:
		- performant enterprise-grade multiplayer canvas
		- deep product and workflow context
		- accumulated design-system and historical product context
		- direct visual manipulation
		- ability to move between AI, design, code, and canvas in one platform
	- **Read-through**: the moat argument is shifting from "design files are collaborative" to "Figma owns the structured context and multiplayer surface where AI-generated software gets refined into shippable product"

---

- ## Internal AI Use
	- Figma is rebuilding work across engineering, go-to-market, and operations around AI tooling
	- Management said it expects flatter organizational structures and smaller, high-agency teams
	- The operating model emphasis is speed and leverage rather than simply adding headcount
	- **Read-through**: Figma is using the same AI-era premise internally that it sells externally: better tools should let smaller teams move faster and ship more

---

- ## Forward-Looking Data Points
	- Around 60% of Figma's largest customers used Make weekly in Q1
	- Over 80% of Make users on full seats continued using Figma Design for visual editing and broader exploration alongside Make
	- MCP weekly active users in Design grew 5x quarter-over-quarter
	- Over 75% of Org and Enterprise users who were above their AI credit limit continued consuming credits after enforcement began
	- Over 95% of the same above-limit users remained active on the platform
	- Pro teams purchasing AI credit add-ons had more seats per team and more than 3x the average annualized spend of teams that had not purchased add-ons
	- Customers using MCP grew full seats roughly 70% faster over the quarter than comparable customers not using MCP
	- NBBJ expects Weave adoption to triple in the next few months
	- **Read-through**: the important metrics to track are Make weekly usage, MCP usage, full-seat upgrades among developers, AI credit overage behavior, and whether Weave expands Figma outside core software-design workflows

---

- ## Read-Through
	- The call was one of the clearest articulations of Figma's AI thesis:
		- code generation lowers the cost of implementation
		- design, taste, and product judgment become more valuable
		- Figma becomes the collaborative surface where AI-generated work is refined, governed, and connected to real product context
	- Make is the most visible AI product, but MCP may be equally important because it connects Figma to developer agents and creates a code-to-canvas loop
	- Weave is strategically important because it suggests Figma can expand from software-product design into broader creative production
	- AI credit monetization is early but gives Figma a second monetization model beyond seats
	- The key risk is that frontier labs and hyperscalers move up the stack from models and code tools into design surfaces
	- The bull case is that Figma's multiplayer canvas, design-system context, workflow history, and direct-manipulation UX are hard to recreate and become more valuable as AI produces more rough software and creative output
