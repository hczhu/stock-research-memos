- **Source**: Cloudflare Q4 2025 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: The upmarket motion is accelerating, and Cloudflare is starting to frame itself as core infrastructure for the agentic internet

---

- ## Products and Positioning
	- Management’s framing shifted toward a **human-workers-to-agents** paradigm
	- The internet and software stack were historically built around **human seats and clicks**
	- In an agentic world, software usage becomes much more intensive: agents visit far more websites, operate continuously, and require guardrails, connectivity, and compute rails
	- Cloudflare argues it is well positioned because it captures value when AI apps are built on **Workers** and when automated traffic increases usage of the broader platform

---

- ## Customers
	- Cloudflare signed the **largest total contract value deal in company history**: **$130M over 5 years**
	- A **U.S. media company** chose Cloudflare after AI scraping drove up network and infrastructure costs
		- Key value proposition: visibility into which AI models are consuming its data
		- Strategic implication: Cloudflare may help customers **protect** and eventually **monetize** unique content
	- A **leading AI company** selected Cloudflare over major hyperscalers after a rigorous RFP

---

- ## Read-Through
	- This quarter strengthens the view that Cloudflare’s AI exposure is not just “sell GPUs more bandwidth”
	- The more interesting angle is **control**: bot management, crawl control, visibility, traffic shaping, and security for automated traffic
	- It also suggests that Cloudflare’s contract sizes are finally starting to look more enterprise-like
