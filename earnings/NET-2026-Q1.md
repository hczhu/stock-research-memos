- **Source**: Cloudflare Q1 2026 earnings call transcript (`net-2026Q1-transcript.pdf`)
- **Theme**: Strong enterprise and AI momentum, a sharp internal shift to an AI-first operating model, and continued proof that Workers is becoming a real growth engine even if it pressures gross margin

---

- ## Headline Metrics
	- Revenue was **$639.8M**, up **34% YoY**
	- Customers paying more than **$100K** annually reached **4,416**, up **25% YoY**
	- Large customers contributed **72% of revenue**, up from **69%** a year ago
	- Dollar-based net retention was **118%**
		- Down **2 points QoQ**
		- Up **7 points YoY**
	- Gross margin was **72.8%**
	- Operating income was **$73.1M**, or **11.4%** operating margin
	- Free cash flow was **$84.1M**, or **13%** of revenue
	- RPO was **$2.543B**, up **36% YoY**
	- Current RPO was **64%** of total RPO and grew **34% YoY**

---

- ## Sales and Customer Momentum
	- Sales productivity improved **year-over-year for the ninth consecutive quarter**
	- Growth in sales-force capacity accelerated at the **fastest pace since 2023**
	- Deals over **$1M** were up **73% YoY**, the fastest growth rate since 2024
	- Cloudflare added a **record number** of customers spending more than **$5M** annually
		- It added as many `$5M+` customers in Q1 as it did in **all of last year**
	- Bookings from new customers increased at the **highest rate since 2023**
	- New pipeline generation grew sequentially at the **fastest pace in five years**
	- Quarterly gross retention reached its **highest level in four years**
	- Management said partners reached **30% of revenue** in the quarter

---

- ## Developer Platform and AI Demand
	- Cloudflare added **1M developers in a single quarter**
	- Developers on the platform increased to more than **5.5M**
		- That compares with an increase of **1.5M in all of 2025**
	- Management said more than **three-quarters** of Workers growth came from **new customers**
	- The company continues to see rapid growth from **AI and agentic workloads** across both the network and the Workers platform
	- Management’s framing is that agentic AI benefits all three acts:
		- **Act 1**: more agentic traffic means more requests, API calls, and commerce traffic across Cloudflare’s network
		- **Act 2**: agents need fine-grained access control, which supports Zero Trust / SASE products
		- **Act 3**: more AI-generated code needs a fast, ephemeral, low-cost place to run, which favors Workers

---

- ## AI-First Operating Model
	- Cloudflare announced a reduction of roughly **1,100+ employees**
		- CFO framed this as approximately **20%** of the workforce
	- Management emphasized this was **not** framed as traditional cost cutting, but as a redesign around an **agentic AI-first operating model**
	- Internally:
		- Cloudflare’s AI usage increased **600%+ in the last three months**
		- **97%** of R&D team members use AI coding tools
		- **100%** of production-code contributions are reviewed by autonomous AI agents
		- Employees across functions run **thousands of AI sessions each day**
		- Internal workflows rely on **dozens of MCP servers** and **hundreds of centrally managed skill files**
	- Cloudflare built an internal harness called **Cloudflare OS**
	- Management said the company is reimagining processes across engineering, finance, and sales to run on an **agentic AI backbone on Workers**

- ## Customers
	- A **leading technology platform** signed a **2-year, $10M pool-of-funds** contract for application services and Workers
		- Cloudflare won workloads from both a **hyperscaler** and point-solution competitors
		- Cloudflare is discussing **AI pay-per-crawl** with this customer
	- A **rapidly growing APAC technology company** signed a **2-year, $8.7M** contract for application services and Workers
		- The company’s growth was driven by AI-powered vibe coding
		- Cloudflare won against a hyperscaler on the strength of its unified platform and low-latency security
	- A **Fortune 100 technology company** signed a **2-year, $8M** contract for **Privacy Proxy**
		- This was the fifth privacy engagement with the customer
		- Cloudflare delivered a working solution in **one week** for precise geolocation handling of user-initiated agentic traffic
	- A **leading insurance company in EMEA** signed a **5-year, $5.1M** contract for application services and the full **SASE** portfolio
		- The customer had over **600 vendors**
		- Cloudflare displaced **6** legacy vendors at signing, with **10** more underway
		- Targeted savings exceeded **$1.3M annually**
	- A **Fortune 500 aerospace and defense company** signed a **3-year, $5M** contract for Zero Trust products
		- Browser Isolation, Access, and Gateway
		- The incumbent browser-isolation product failed critical government compliance requirements
	- A **leading AI company** signed a **1-year, $4.1M** contract for application services
		- Despite a strong build-over-buy mentality, it chose Cloudflare’s security layer
		- The customer is already testing **AI Gateway**
	- Another **leading AI company** signed a **10-month, $2M** contract for **Argo Smart Routing**
		- This came one quarter after signing a Workers deal
		- Argo reduced average global latency by **30%**

- ## Margins, Mix, and Infrastructure
	- Gross margin declined because a larger share of network traffic became **paid** rather than free traffic
		- This shifts cost allocation from sales and marketing into cost of revenue
	- Workers products still run at **lower gross margin** than the corporate average
	- Management’s argument is that gross margin alone is becoming a less useful lens than **operating margin** or broader **unit economics**
	- Network CapEx was **9% of revenue** in Q1
	- Full-year 2026 network CapEx is expected to be **14% to 15%** of revenue
	- Management argued that Cloudflare’s GPU economics differ from hyperscalers because it focuses on **utilization and scheduling efficiency**, not simply leasing servers at marked-up rates
	- Management said GPU utilization is approaching CPU-style utilization and implied it is materially better than hyperscaler utilization

- ## Read-Through
	- This was one of Cloudflare’s most important narrative quarters in years.
	- The business is clearly improving on traditional metrics:
		- better pipeline
		- faster large-deal growth
		- stronger retention
		- more large customers
	- But the more important change is strategic:
		- Cloudflare is trying to turn itself into the first large internet-infrastructure company that is structurally organized around AI agents, not just selling AI tools to others
	- The biggest near-term investor debate will likely be whether this AI-first restructuring is a genuine productivity unlock or an overaggressive narrative move.
	- The strongest product takeaway is that **Workers is now clearly real**, even if it still dilutes gross margin.
	- The strongest competitive takeaway is that Cloudflare keeps winning AI-related workloads for **security, routing, privacy, crawl control, and developer execution**, which looks more durable than trying to compete head-on with hyperscalers on generic inference capacity.
