- **Source**: Roblox 2026 Q1 earnings call and filings
- **Theme**: Age-up — the age verification rollout is simultaneously a near-term bookings headwind and the foundation for long-term monetization improvement

---

- ## Headline
	- Cut full-year bookings growth guidance to **8–12%**
	- 18–34 users grew **50% YoY** and monetize **50% higher** than under-18s in the same quarter
	- O18 players account for **80% of the global gaming market** (~$150B excluding China)

---

- ## Growth Headwinds
	- Age-check rollout (gated chat globally in January) restricted on-platform communication for non-age-checked users and diluted communication for age-checked users
		- Impact is at top of funnel: existing users are fine; new user acquisition slowed
		- Sign-up headwind over the last few months will pressure bookings over the remainder of the year
	- Content discovery and recommendation were too monetization-biased — fixed for better user experience
	- Platform ban in Russia took effect December 2025

---

- ## Product Features
	- **Chat**: Features to balance age-check communication boundaries
		- Global chat across servers
		- Party chat native (Discord-style)
		- Preset messages
	- **Roblox Reality**: New foundation for creation and play combining hyperscale multiplayer, photo-realism, and human-like NPCs into a single unified reality
		- Hybrid architecture blending Roblox Cloud, Game Engine, and Video World Models
		- Defines a new technical frontier: photorealism + hyperscale multiplayer + human-like NPCs on global edge-computing infrastructure
	- **Incentives for O18 novel games**
		- 37.8% DevEx rate for in-game spend from age-checked O18 users
		- Games must utilize R15 avatar framework
	- **Studio Partnerships**: Working with well-known game studios to bring reimagined versions of beloved PC/mobile games to Roblox; total audience of 150M+ monthly players

---

- ## Short-Term Monetization vs Long-Term Sustainability (central tension)
	- **Explicit re-prioritization of discovery toward retention, not monetization** — the clearest statement of the tension:
		- > "As we push towards 10% of gaming, we believe our discovery algorithms should be focused primarily on driving incremental long-term platform retention over short-term monetization, especially to grow our 18 and up user base. We're implementing this transition now."
		- Discovery is being re-tuned to optimize for **28-day retention and beyond**, organically rewarding **high-quality, long-retention experiences** over short-term-monetization games.
	- **The monetization bias was self-inflicting damage on the funnel** — management's own diagnosis of why sign-ups slowed:
		- > "the monetization bias of our recommendation engine likely negatively impacted app store ratings and ultimately sign-ups."
		- Discovery that was "weighted more towards monetization" (compounded by age-check comms reduction) → lower app-store ratings → reduced organic sign-ups. So the short-term-monetization tilt was *costing* top-of-funnel growth, not just risking it.
	- **Creator incentives are being aligned to durability, not extraction**:
		- Baszucki: want to drive 18+ user growth "even if it is slightly less weighting short-term [monetization]"; creators "want to really invest in long-term retaining high-quality properties, and it's somewhat frustrating for them when they might see a shorter-term monetization type game that really isn't going to be around for a long time." Concludes it is right "both intuitively as well as systemically."
		- **Systems-company / trust-based economy**: DevEx is a closed-loop virtuous cycle; the rate is raised slowly so creators *trust* they'll earn → invest more effort → higher-quality, durable content. O18 age-checked DevEx raised **26.6% → 37.8%** (eff. Jun 8) to reward **novel, high-quality, durable** games (must use R15) — monetizing the higher-value 18+ cohort *through quality*, not spend prompts.
	- **Safety framed as a compounding moat that must be paid for up front**:
		- > "We've always viewed safety as a compounding moat for Roblox … But it does mean continued friction in the short term."
		- Strategic upside is "the right thing to do for the long-term health of the platform"; "we're committed to the long view"; long-term growth "continues to be our North Star."
	- **Business-model diversification toward more sustainable monetization** (away from repeated Robux spend-prompts):
		- **Roblox Reality "will not be free"** — a subscription/usage-paid premium tier (real-time photoreal video models; inference funded by usage). Plus studio partnerships and premium/novel O18 experiences — broadening monetization beyond dopamine-driven microtransactions.
	- **Democratization vs curation balance** (Gawrelski's "tension between content safety and democratizing the platform"):
		- Age-check *enables* the balance: align content to age, give kids/select accounts a "very, very large" curated corpus, open UGC for 16+ to innovate while restricting what reaches under-16. Democratization tracked via growth rates of creator 1 / 10 / 100 / **1,000** (the 1,000th growing fastest = broadening base).
	- **The quantified short-term cost of the trade-off** (the price management is willing to pay): FY guidance cut to **20–25% revenue / 8–12% bookings**; DAUs contract Q1→Q2, recover in Q3; **~1/4 of the margin reduction** is incremental **AI + 18-over DevEx investment** — explicitly funding the long-term platform, with future investments to be funded by operating leverage.
	- **Read-through**: this is the financial embodiment of the [[2026-06-10-roblox-age-based-experiences-parent-safety-perspective]] / [[2026-06-08-roblox-aging-up-thesis]] thesis — Roblox is deliberately sacrificing near-term bookings (monetization-biased discovery, dopamine mechanics, frictionless sign-ups) to build a safer, higher-quality, higher-LTV (18+) ecosystem. The bet's success hinges on whether retention-optimized discovery + age-gated trust actually compound into durable engagement before the bookings headwind tests investor patience.
