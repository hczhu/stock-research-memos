- tags:: [[Cloudflare]], [[NET]], [[SaaS]], [[networking]], [[security]], [[developer-tools]], [[earnings]]

- **Source**: Cloudflare Q1 2022 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Traffic and CPU growth show the network is becoming more intelligent, not just bigger

---

- ## Network Metrics
	- Traffic on Cloudflare’s network increased **75.8% YoY**
	- Traffic was up **15.9% sequentially**
	- CPU utilization grew **89.1% YoY**
	- CPU utilization was up **21.8% sequentially**
	- Read-through: compute-intensive products like **Workers** and security are growing faster than raw bandwidth

---

- ## Hiring and Culture
	- Leadership cited receiving **133K job applications** in the quarter
	- The company hired **310 employees**

---

- ## Product and Market Notes
	- Management highlighted **network and security vendor consolidation** as a meaningful tailwind
	- **Email security** was called a major blind spot among leading Zero Trust vendors
	- R2 was positioned at **$0.015/GB/month** with **no egress fees**
	- The source compares that with AWS S3 at roughly **$0.021/GB/month** plus egress
	- A rough thought experiment in the deck suggests that **200 PB** on R2 could imply roughly **$36M annual revenue**

---

- ## Customers
	- A **Fortune 1000 gaming company** signed a **$3.3M, 3-year** contract after AWS security failed to keep its app online under attack
	- A **Fortune 1000 trucking company** signed a **$385K, 2-year** deal for **7,500 seats**, adding email security
	- A **large Indian company** signed a **$150K** Zero Trust deal for **5,000 seats**
	- A **Midwest U.S. state government** signed a **$5.1M, 3-year** deal
	- A **large social network** signed a **$3M, 5-year** Workers deal for messaging authentication
	- A **major Australian software company** signed an initial **$145K** Workers + Durable Objects engagement

---

- ## Read-Through
	- This quarter is one of the cleanest data-point quarters in the source deck
	- CPU growth faster than traffic growth is especially important because it supports the thesis that Cloudflare is monetizing intelligence, not just bit delivery
