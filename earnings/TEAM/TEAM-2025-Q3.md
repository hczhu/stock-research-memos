- **Source**: Atlassian FY2026 Q1 earnings (calendar Q3 2025, quarter ended September 2025)
- **Theme**: AI coding tools are expanding Jira seats; Data Center end-of-life announced with Atlassian Ascend migration path; government cloud milestones

---

- ## Headline
	- Customers using AI coding tools (Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor) expand Jira seats **~5% faster** than those who don't
	- **50% of users** of core products are from business teams (HR, marketing, legal, finance) — not just engineers/product
	- Announced end of **Data Center** offering; introduced **Atlassian Ascend** to help customers upgrade to Cloud over coming years
	- Data Center = ~25% of current revenue (migration to Cloud = multi-year revenue repricing opportunity)

---

- ## Products
	- **Atlassian Ascend**: New migration program to help Data Center customers transition to Cloud ahead of DC end-of-life (license sales end 2028; subscriptions expire / go read-only 2029)
	- **Atlassian Isolated Cloud (Early Access)**: Air-gapped cloud environment for sensitive enterprise use cases
	- **Atlassian Government Cloud (General Availability)**: Opens U.S. federal and regulated public sector market; FedRAMP High and IL5 compliance
	- **Confluence scaling**: Cloud roadmap to support up to 250,000 users per instance (removes a key objection for large enterprise)
	- **The Dia browser acquisition** (Browser Company acquisition closed): AI browser from the company behind Arc; intended as AI-native entry point to work

---

- ## Customers
	- **AI coding correlation**: Cohort analysis found customers using GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor, and Replit expand paid Jira seats ~5% higher than non-AI-tool users (Rovo Dev excluded) — AI makes Atlassian stickier, not a threat
	- **Business team penetration**: 50% of core product users are non-technical business teams (HR, marketing, legal, finance) — validates the "System of Work" expansion thesis beyond developer tools

- ## Data Points & Context
	- On-prem sunsetting timeline confirmed:
		- **March 2026**: New customers can no longer buy Data Center licenses
		- **March 2028**: End of license sales and expansions for existing customers
		- **March 2029**: DC subscriptions expire; instances become read-only
	- Price increases effective October 2025: Jira Standard +5%, Premium +7.5%, Enterprise +7.5-10%; Confluence Standard +5%, Premium +7.5%, Enterprise +7.5-10%; JSM Standard +5%, Premium/Enterprise +7.5%
