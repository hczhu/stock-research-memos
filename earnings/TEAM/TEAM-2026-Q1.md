- **Source**: Atlassian FY2026 Q3 earnings (calendar Q1 2026, quarter ended March 2026)
- **Theme**: First GAAP operating profit + Service Collection hits $1B ARR; Rovo agent orchestration opens new enterprise workflow category

---

- ## Headline
	- GAAP operating margin expected ~**4.5%** — first time ever GAAP profitable on an operating basis
	- Non-GAAP op margin trend: 34% → 27% → **23%** over past 3 quarters (deliberate R&D investment)
	- **Service Collection** (JSM + CSM + Assets + Rovo) crossed **$1B ARR**, growing >30% YoY, 65,000 customers including 50% of F500
	- **Largest ever quarter** for competitive displacements from a major ITSM provider (believed to be ServiceNow)
	- **NRR > 120%** — seat expansion + cross-sell working
	- Two primary revenue beat drivers: cross-sell and seat expansion

---

- ## Strategies
	- Three key strategic priorities reinforced:
		- **Enterprise** (move up-market)
		- **AI** (Rovo and agent orchestration)
		- **System of Work** (unified platform across Collections)

---

- ## Products
	- **Agent Orchestration in Jira**: Jira becomes the place to orchestrate work across humans and AI agents; teams can assign tasks to Rovo and third-party AI agents, collaborate via comments, integrate agents into workflows with full audit trails and admin governance; open toolchain supports MCP-enabled agents alongside Rovo
	- **Customer Service Management (CSM)**: New app built on the same AI-native foundation as JSM; purpose-built for external customer support vs. JSM's internal IT focus; bridges front-line support agents with technical teams already in Jira; part of Service Collection
	- **Rovo Service GA**: Human-supervised AI agent that plans and executes employee support workflows (ticket resolution, HR onboarding); automatically routes, answers, and acts across systems by pulling context from existing knowledge, past tickets, and company policies
	- **Proactive AIOps**: AI-driven early incident detection and change risk assessment; helps IT ops get ahead of problems instead of reacting; integrated into JSM

---

- ## Customers
	- Service Collection ($1B ARR, >30% YoY, 65K customers, 50% F500) — the enterprise ITSM flywheel is working
	- This quarter was the largest ever for competitive displacements from a major ITSM provider
	- Customers using AI code generation tools (GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor, Replit) expand paid Jira seats at a rate ~5% higher than those who don't (even excluding Rovo Dev)
