- tags:: [[Cloudflare]], [[NET]], [[SaaS]], [[networking]], [[security]], [[developer-tools]], [[earnings]]

- **Source**: Cloudflare Q2 2022 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Revenue mix keeps moving upmarket, while Workers and Zero Trust continue to differentiate the platform

---

- ## Revenue Mix
	- Pay-as-you-go was only **11% of revenue**
	- That share had declined from **14% in 2021**
	- The mix shift supports the view that Cloudflare is steadily moving away from SMB dependence

---

- ## Customers
	- A **Fortune 500 industrial company** signed a **$1.3M, 5-year** upsell
	- The **State of Arizona** signed a **$770K, 1-year** expansion
	- One of the world’s largest advertising conglomerates signed a **$1.7M, 1-year** deal after first engaging under attack the prior quarter
	- One of the largest online recruiting firms signed a **$5.5M, 3-year** deal after a highly technical evaluation
	- A public security compliance vendor, a leading endpoint-security provider, and one of the largest data-security vendors each signed multi-year contracts worth more than **$700K**
	- Cloudflare signed up **half of Zscaler’s top channel partners** as new Cloudflare partners in Q2

---

- ## Workers
	- Cloudflare signed deals with a large e-commerce platform, a fast-growing web development platform, and a next-generation database platform to embed **Workers as a service**
	- Management emphasized Workers efficiency
	- The source deck cites that **Cloudflare Workers can cost about 3x less than AWS Lambda** for an equivalent workload
		- Lambda: **$1.84 per million CPU requests**
		- Workers: **$0.50 per million CPU requests**

---

- ## Read-Through
	- The quarter is one of the clearest examples of the thesis that Cloudflare’s broad platform and ease of use can beat best-of-breed competitors in real evaluations
