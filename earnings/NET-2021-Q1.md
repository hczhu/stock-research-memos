- **Source**: Cloudflare Q1 2021 earnings materials cited in `Cloudflare $NET - Investment thesis.pdf`
- **Theme**: Disciplined execution and long-horizon incubation remain core parts of the culture

---

- ## Culture
	- CEO Matthew Prince repeated a long-held management idea: keep the company well maintained and keep “the nose 10 degrees above the horizon”
	- Cloudflare was designed for **consistent and disciplined execution**
	- Roughly **10%** of the team was dedicated to `ETI` (Emerging Technology and Incubations)
	- ETI’s job was to invent products and features that would expand TAM over a **two-year** horizon
	- Management cited **Cloudflare for Teams** and **Workers** as examples of ETI-generated expansion

---

- ## Product and Regulatory Context
	- Data locality and residency requirements in countries such as India, Brazil, and Germany were becoming more important
	- This trend can benefit Cloudflare because customers need infrastructure that can satisfy a patchwork of regulatory environments

---

- ## Read-Through
	- The quarter reinforces that Cloudflare’s culture is not purely reactive
	- The long-horizon R&D structure is central to the bull case because it explains how the company keeps opening adjacent categories
